

Understanding the **problems of goal formulation** helps you diagnose why organizations struggle to set effective goals—even when they know the SMART framework and have good intentions.

Here are the major problems, organized by category.

---

## Major Problems in Goal Formulation

### Category 1: Problems Related to Goal Content (What the goals are)

| Problem                 | Description                                                                               | Example                                                                                                                                                                  |
| :---------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vagueness**           | Goals are stated in abstract, unmeasurable language.                                      | "We will strive for excellence." (What does excellence mean? How will we know when we achieve it?)                                                                       |
| **Too many goals**      | Organizations try to pursue dozens of goals simultaneously, diluting focus and resources. | A startup lists 25 goals for the next quarter: increase sales, improve morale, redesign website, hire 10 people, cut costs, launch 3 products... Nothing gets done well. |
| **Conflicting goals**   | Two or more goals pull the organization in opposite directions.                           | "Maximize customer satisfaction" (spend more on service) vs. "Minimize operating costs" (cut service budget).                                                            |
| **Too difficult goals** | Goals are impossible given current resources, time, or technology.                        | "Become the market leader in 3 months" when the current market leader has 80% share and 20 years of advantage. Leads to frustration, cheating, or resignation.           |


### Category 2: Problems Related to the Formulation Process (How goals are set)

| Problem                      | Description                                                                                                                                           | Example                                                                                                                                                                        |
| :--------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Top-management isolation** | Leaders set goals without input from those who do the work or serve customers.                                                                        | CEO announces "Cut costs by 30%" without knowing that the customer service team is already understaffed. Quality collapses.                                                    |
| **Lack of participation**    | Employees whose performance will be measured by the goals had no voice in creating them.                                                              | Factory workers are given a "zero defects" goal but were never asked if the machines are capable of it. Resentment and sabotage follow.                                        |
| **Political infighting**     | Different departments or executives push for goals that benefit themselves, not the organization.                                                     | Marketing wants a "brand awareness" goal. Finance wants a "cost reduction" goal. R&D wants an "innovation" goal. No agreement. Goals become a compromise that serves no one.   |
| **Lack of alignment**        | Goals at different levels (corporate, department, individual) contradict each other.                                                                  | Corporate goal: "Improve customer satisfaction." Individual sales goal: "Close 50 deals per week." To close deals fast, salespeople pressure customers, reducing satisfaction. |

### Category 3: Problems Related to Goal Communication

| Problem                  | Description                                                                                                          | Example                                                                                                                                                    |
| :----------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Poor cascading**       | Strategic goals are never translated into operational goals. Employees have no idea what they should do differently. | CEO announces "We will become digital-first." A customer service agent thinks: "Does that mean I should answer emails before phone calls? No one told me." |
| **Information overload** | So many goals are communicated (often in a 50-page document) that no one remembers or focuses on the critical few.   | An employee receives a 10-page list of 40 annual goals. By March, they remember only 2.                                                                    |

### Category 4: Problems Related to Goal Acceptance & Commitment

| Problem                    | Description                                                                            | Example                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rejection**              | Employees actively resist the goal because they disagree with it or see it as harmful. | A hospital sets a goal of "discharge patients within 48 hours." Nurses know this is unsafe for post-surgery patients. They deliberately work slowly to prevent early discharges. |
| **Passive non-compliance** | Employees agree publicly but do nothing to achieve the goal privately.                 | During the meeting: "Yes, sir, we will increase sales by 20%." After the meeting: Nothing changes.                                                                               |

### Category 5: Problems Related to the External Environment

| Problem | Description | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Environmental turbulence** | Goals become obsolete before they can be achieved because the market, technology, or regulations change. | A travel company sets a goal to "expand to 50 new destinations in 2020." Then COVID-19 hits. The goal is now irrelevant. |
| **Uncertainty** | Goals are based on assumptions that may be wrong. | "We will capture 30% market share by launching Product X for $100." But a competitor launches a better product at $80 the same week. |
| **Resource volatility** | Resources needed to achieve goals (budget, staff, materials) are unpredictable. | A nonprofit sets a goal to "build 100 wells this year." Donations suddenly drop by 60%. The goal cannot be met. |

---

## The Interconnection: How Problems Compound

These problems rarely occur in isolation. They feed into each other:

```text
Top-management isolation
         ↓
Leaders set unrealistic goals (too difficult)
         ↓
Poor communication → Employees don't understand the goals
         ↓
No acceptance → Passive non-compliance
         ↓
Poor performance
         ↓
Leaders set even more unrealistic goals (cycle worsens)
         ↓
Goal displacement (people focus on measurable rules, not purpose)
```

## Real-World Example: Multiple Problems at Once

**Scenario:** A retail chain announces: "Become the #1 retailer in customer satisfaction within 6 months."

| Problem Type | Specific Problem in This Scenario |
| :--- | :--- |
| Vagueness | What does "#1" mean? Which survey? What score? |
| Too difficult | The current #1 has held the position for 10 years. |
| Short-term focus | 6 months is unrealistic for cultural change. |
| Lack of participation | Store employees (who interact with customers) were never consulted. |
| Poor cascading | A cashier thinks: "Does this mean I should ignore the express lane rule and chat with every customer?" |
| Conflicting goals | The chain also has a goal to "reduce labor costs" (fewer staff per store). Fewer staff = longer wait times = lower satisfaction. |

**Result:** The goal fails. Employees are confused and cynical. The chain abandons the goal after 6 months and never revisits customer satisfaction.

## Quick Revision Card

| Concept | One-Line Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Problems of goal formulation** | Obstacles that prevent organizations from creating effective, actionable goals that drive performance. |
| **Most common single problem** | **Vagueness** (failing the SMART test). |
| **Most dangerous problem** | **Conflicting goals** (pulls the organization apart) or **Mixed messages** (rewards contradict stated goals). |
| **Prevention** | SMART criteria + participation (MBO) + alignment checks + periodic review + flexibility. |
