
The **purpose** of an organization answers the fundamental question: **Why does this organization exist?** It is broader and more philosophical than goals.

Purposes are often captured in two concepts:

#### A. Mission (External Purpose)
- **What it is:** The organization’s reason for existence from the customer’s or society’s perspective.
- **Question it answers:** "What value do we provide to the outside world?"
- **Example:** *"Disney’s purpose is to make people happy."* (Not just to sell movie tickets).

#### B. Vision (Internal Direction)
- **What it is:** The future the organization aspires to create.
- **Question it answers:** "What do we want to become?"
- **Example:** *"Tesla’s purpose is to accelerate the world’s transition to sustainable energy."*

**How Purpose Differs from Goals:**

| Aspect | Purpose | Goals |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Timeframe** | Enduring (decades, lifetime) | Shorter-term (months to years) |
| **Measurability** | Abstract, qualitative | Concrete, quantitative |
| **Changeability** | Rarely changes | Changes as targets are met |
| **Example** | "To serve humanity's communication needs." | "Launch a 5G network in 6 cities by Dec." |

**The Simple Relationship:**

> **Purpose** (Why we exist) → **Mission** (What we do) → **Goals** (Specific targets we aim for)
