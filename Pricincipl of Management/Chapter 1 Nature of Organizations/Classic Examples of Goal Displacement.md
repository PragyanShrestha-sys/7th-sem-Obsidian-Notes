**Example 1: The Bureaucratic Office**
- **Original goal:** Issue passports to citizens efficiently.
- **Procedure created:** Require original birth certificate, two photos, and fee.
- **Displacement:** Clerk rejects an application because the photo is 1mm too large, even though the citizen is clearly identifiable. The clerk says, "Rules are rules." The goal of serving the citizen is lost.

**Example 2: The Sales Team**
- **Original goal:** Build long-term customer relationships and maximize lifetime value.
- **Metric:** Number of calls made per day.
- **Displacement:** Salespeople make 100 meaningless 30-second calls per day, annoying prospects and closing no deals. They hit the "call count" metric but fail the real goal.

**Example 3: The Charity (Very Common)**
- **Original goal:** Feed hungry children.
- **Metric:** Money raised from donors.
- **Displacement:** The charity spends 90% of its budget on expensive fundraising events and celebrity dinners to raise more money, but only 10% actually buys food for children. The goal shifted from "feeding children" to "raising money."
