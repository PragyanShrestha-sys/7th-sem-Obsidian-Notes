## [[Concept of Organizations and  Organization Goals(concept, purposes and types]]

An organization is not just a building or a group of people. In management theory, it is defined as a **consciously coordinated social unit, composed of two or more people, that functions on a relatively continuous basis to achieve a common goal or set of goals.**

## [[Features of effective organizational growth (S.M.A.R.T)]]

| Letter | Feature    | Key Question                                     |
| :----- | :--------- | :----------------------------------------------- |
| **S**  | Specific   | What exactly do we want to accomplish?           |
| **M**  | Measurable | How will we know we've succeeded?                |
| **A**  | Achievable | Can we realistically do this with our resources? |
| **R**  | Relevant   | Does this align with our purpose?                |
| **T**  | Time-bound | By when must this be done?                       |

## [[Goal Succession and Goal displacement]]

| Concept               | One-Line Definition                                                      | Key Sign                                                                            | Manager Action                                                           |
| :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Goal Succession**   | Achieving a goal and replacing it with a new, higher one.                | Organization keeps growing and setting new targets.                                 | Encourage planning for the next goal before the current one is achieved. |
| **Goal Displacement** | Forgetting the real purpose and becoming obsessed with rules or metrics. | Employees say, "That's not my job" or "Rules are rules" when a customer needs help. | Regularly audit whether means still serve ends.                          |

| Term                  | Memory Trick                                                                                                 |
| :-------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Goal Succession**   | *"Next goal!"* — Like a runner crossing the finish line and immediately aiming for the next race.            |
| **Goal Displacement** | *"Lost the plot."* — Like studying so hard for an exam's format that you forget to learn the actual subject. |

## [[Changing Prespectives of organization]]

| Perspective      | Core Metaphor | Key Focus                           | Why Shift Occurred                             |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Mechanistic      | Machine       | Efficiency, control, predictability | Industrial need for order over chaos           |
| Open System      | Organism      | Adaptation, environment interaction | Mechanistic failed in changing markets         |
| Political System | Arena         | Power, conflict, negotiation        | Open system ignored internal competition       |
| Culture          | Tribe/Society | Shared meaning, values, rituals     | Political system ignored unity & identity      |
| Learning System  | Brain         | Continuous adaptation, knowledge    | Culture alone couldn’t drive deliberate change |
