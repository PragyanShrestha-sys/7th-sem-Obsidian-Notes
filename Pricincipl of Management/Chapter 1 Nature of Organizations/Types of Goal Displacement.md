| Type                          | Description                                                                                             | Example                                                                                                                                                              |
| :---------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Rule displacement**         | Following rules becomes more important than achieving the original purpose.                             | A hospital's goal is "patient health." But staff focuses on "filling out all forms correctly" even if it delays treatment.                                           |
| **Measurement displacement**  | Measuring and hitting the metric becomes more important than what the metric was supposed to represent. | A call center's goal is "customer satisfaction." But they measure "average call time." Agents rush customers off the phone to keep time low, making customers angry. |
| **Bureaucratic displacement** | Protecting one's department, budget, or power becomes the goal, not serving the organization's mission. | A university's goal is "education." But a department fights to keep outdated courses because they protect faculty jobs.                                              |
| **Success displacement**      | Celebrating and protecting past successes prevents pursuing new, better goals.                          | Kodak's goal was "film photography." They achieved dominance. But that success prevented them from embracing digital cameras, leading to bankruptcy.                 |
