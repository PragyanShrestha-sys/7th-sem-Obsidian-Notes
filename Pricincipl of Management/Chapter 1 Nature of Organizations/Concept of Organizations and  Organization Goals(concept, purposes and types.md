

### 1. Concept of an Organization

An organization is not just a building or a group of people. In management theory, it is defined as a **consciously coordinated social unit, composed of two or more people, that functions on a relatively continuous basis to achieve a common goal or set of goals.**

**Key Characteristics of an Organization:**

- **Social Entity:** It consists of people who interact with each other (not machines or software alone).
- **Goal-Directed:** Everything done within it is aimed at achieving specific outcomes.
- **Deliberate Structure:** There are defined roles, responsibilities, and relationships (e.g., hierarchy, departments).
- **Coordinated Activity:** Work is divided among members and then re-integrated to ensure efficiency.
- **Boundary:** It can distinguish between members (employees) and non-members (customers, public).
- **Continuity:** It exists beyond the tenure of any single individual.

> **Example:** A local restaurant is an organization. It has a manager, chefs, waitstaff, and cleaners (social entity). They follow a duty roster and recipes (deliberate structure) to serve meals profitably (goal), day after day (continuity).

### 2. Organizational Goals

**Organizational goals** are the desired future states or outcomes that an organization seeks to achieve. They are the "ends" toward which organizational efforts are directed.

**Purpose of Goals:**
- **Guide action** (what to do and what not to do).
- **Set standards** for measuring performance.
- **Motivate members** by providing a clear target.
- **Legitimize the organization's existence** to outsiders (investors, society).

**Types of Goals (Important for exams):**

| Type | Focus | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Official Goals** (Mission) | Broad, abstract, public statements of purpose. | "To maximize shareholder value." |
| **Operative Goals** (Specific) | Measurable, short-term targets for daily operations. | "Increase sales by 10% this quarter." |
| **Strategic Goals** | Long-term, company-wide objectives (3-5 years). | "Expand into three new international markets." |
| **Financial Goals** | Related to profits, costs, revenue. | "Achieve a 15% return on investment." |
| **Social Goals** | Community, environmental, ethical responsibilities. | "Reduce carbon emissions by 30% by 2030." |

> **Note:** Goals often exist in a **hierarchy**. The CEO sets strategic goals; middle managers translate them into operative goals for teams.

### Summary Table for Revision

| Concept | Core Idea | Key Question |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Organization** | A group of people working together with a planned structure. | *How are we structured to work together?* |
| **Organizational Goals** | Specific, measurable targets to achieve within a time period. | *What specific results must we hit?* |
| **Organizational Purpose** | The fundamental, ongoing reason for existence. | *Why do we exist at all?* |

### Exam Tip
If asked a question like *"Can an organization exist without a purpose?"* the answer is **no**. Without a purpose, there is no basis for setting goals, no reason for coordination, and no way to measure success—it would just be a random crowd of people.
