Based on the image file you provided, here is a **short explanation** of the 5 problems of goal formulation listed in your material:

## 1. Improper Reward System
- **Meaning:** When rewards (bonuses, promotions, recognition) are not clearly linked to achieving goals.
- **Problem:** Employees lose motivation because they don't see any personal benefit from reaching the goal.

## 2. Environmental Constraint
- **Meaning:** External factors like technology changes, new laws, political shifts, or economic conditions.
- **Problem:** Future is unpredictable. Goals set today may become unrealistic or irrelevant if the environment changes unexpectedly.

## 3. Unwillingness of Managers in Goal Formulation
- **Meaning:** Managers avoid participating in setting goals.
- **Problem:** Lack of confidence, experience, or knowledge makes managers hesitant. Without their input, goals may be impractical or ignored.

## 4. Resistance to Change
- **Meaning:** Employees and managers fear the changes that goals require.
- **Problem:** Fear of job loss, inability to learn new skills, or comfort with the current situation leads to active or passive resistance against goal formulation.

## 5. Resource Constraint
- **Meaning:** Limited availability of money, staff, time, equipment, or technology.
- **Problem:** Goals formulated without realistically assessing available resources are doomed to fail from the start.
