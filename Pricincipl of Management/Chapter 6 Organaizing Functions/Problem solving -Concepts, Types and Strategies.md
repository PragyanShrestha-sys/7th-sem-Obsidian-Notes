## Part 1: Concepts of Problem Solving (Quick Recap)

### What is a Problem?

A **problem** exists when there is a **gap** between the **current state** (what is happening) and the **desired state** (what should be happening).

| Term | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Current state** | Actual situation now | Production = 80 units per day |
| **Desired state** | Goal or target | Production = 100 units per day |
| **Problem** | The gap between them | Shortfall of 20 units per day |

### What is Problem Solving?

**Problem solving** is the process of identifying this gap, finding the root cause, generating and implementing solutions, and evaluating the results.

> **In simple words:** Problem solving = finding out why things are not as they should be, and then fixing them.

## Part 3: [[Problem Solving Strategies]]

**Problem solving strategies** are the overall approaches or plans managers use to tackle problems. Different strategies work for different types of problems.

Here are the **major problem solving strategies**:

| Strategy                    | Description                                                        | Best for                             |
| :-------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------- |
| **1. Algorithm**            | Step-by-step procedure that guarantees a solution                  | Well-structured, routine problems    |
| **2. Heuristic**            | "Rule of thumb" based on experience (not guaranteed, but quick)    | Routine problems, time pressure      |
| **3. Trial and Error**      | Trying different solutions until one works                         | Simple problems, low risk            |
| **4. Brainstorming**        | Group generates many ideas without criticism                       | Creative solutions, complex problems |
| **5. Root Cause Analysis**  | Finding the underlying cause, not just treating symptoms           | Recurring problems, complex problems |
| **6. Divide and Conquer**   | Breaking a large problem into smaller, manageable parts            | Large, complex problems              |
| **7. Means-Ends Analysis**  | Comparing current state to goal and taking steps to reduce the gap | Planning, goal-oriented problems     |
| **8. Analogical Reasoning** | Using solutions from similar past problems                         | Familiar problems in new contexts    |

## Summary Table: Problem Types vs. Best Strategies (Exam-Ready)

| Type of Problem | Best Strategy | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Well-structured, routine** | Algorithm, Heuristic | Clear steps, fast |
| **Ill-structured, complex** | Root Cause Analysis, Brainstorming | Need to understand cause; need creative ideas |
| **Crisis, urgent** | Heuristic, Intuition | No time for analysis |
| **Large, complex** | Divide and Conquer | Break into manageable pieces |
| **Recurring problem** | Root Cause Analysis | Prevent it from happening again |
| **Goal-oriented** | Means-Ends Analysis | Focus on reducing the gap |
| **Similar to past problem** | Analogical Reasoning | Use what worked before |
| **Simple, low-risk** | Trial and Error | Experiment until solution found |
