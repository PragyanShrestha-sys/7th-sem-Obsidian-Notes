## [[Concept and functions of leadership]]

**In simple words:** Leadership is the process of influencing, guiding, and directing the behavior of people in an organization to willingly work towards the achievement of group goals. 

**The Golden Distinction (Never confuse these in your exam!):**

- **Management:** Focuses on *things*—plans, budgets, structures, control. (Gets people to do the *right things* efficiently).
- **Leadership:** Focuses on *people*—vision, inspiration, motivation, change. (Gets people to *want* to do the right things).

## [[Types of leadership]]

| Style                                        | What it means                                                                                                                                                                                                         | When to use it                                                                                                                                                                  |
| :------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **1. Autocratic (Authoritarian) Leadership** | The leader makes all decisions unilaterally. They tell subordinates **what** to do, **how** to do it, and **when** to do it, with zero input from the team. Communication is strictly top-down.                       | • In a crisis/emergency (e.g., fire evacuation). <br> • When employees are unskilled or inexperienced. <br> • When strict compliance is required (e.g., factory assembly line). |
| **2. Democratic (Participative) Leadership** | The leader involves subordinates in the decision-making process. They consult the team, listen to ideas, and encourage participation. However, the *final* decision still rests with the leader.                      | • When the team is skilled and knowledgeable. <br> • When you need creative solutions and buy-in. <br> • When there is enough time to discuss options.                          |
| **3. Laissez-Faire (Free-Rein) Leadership**  | The leader takes a "hands-off" approach. They provide the necessary resources and tools, and then give the team complete freedom to make decisions and execute tasks on their own. The leader only steps in if asked. | • When the team consists of highly qualified experts (e.g., R&D scientists, senior doctors). <br> • When the leader trusts the team completely.                                 |

## [[Formation of groups]]
| Stage             | Emotion           | Key Behavior             | Leader's Style               | Risk                                                |
| :---------------- | :---------------- | :----------------------- | :--------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Forming**    | Anxious, Excited  | Polite, Dependent        | Telling (Directive)          | Lack of clarity                                     |
| **2. Storming**   | Angry, Frustrated | Arguing, Resisting       | Selling (Coaching)           | Group may break up!                                 |
| **3. Norming**    | Trusting, Calm    | Cooperating, Helping     | Participating (Facilitating) | Group may become too cozy and avoid tough decisions |
| **4. Performing** | Confident, Proud  | Productive, Autonomous   | Delegating (Observing)       | External threats (budget cuts, politics)            |
| **5. Adjourning** | Nostalgic, Sad    | Wrapping up, Celebrating | Supporting (Counseling)      | Loss of key talent
