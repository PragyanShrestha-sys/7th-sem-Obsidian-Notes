## What is SWOT Analysis? (Definition)

**SWOT Analysis** is a strategic planning tool that identifies and evaluates the **internal Strengths and Weaknesses** and **external Opportunities and Threats** of an organization. It provides a clear, structured summary of the factors that affect an organization's ability to achieve its goals.

> **In simple words:** SWOT tells you what you are good at, what you are bad at, what chances you can grab, and what dangers you must avoid.

### The Acronym

| Letter | Meaning | Environment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S** | **Strengths** | Internal (Positive) |
| **W** | **Weaknesses** | Internal (Negative) |
| **O** | **Opportunities** | External (Positive) |
| **T** | **Threats** | External (Negative) |

## Part 1: Strengths (Internal Positive)

### What are Strengths?

**Strengths** are the internal capabilities, resources, and attributes that give an organization an **advantage** over others. They are things the organization does well or unique assets it possesses.

### Examples of Strengths

| Category | Examples |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Resources** | Strong financial position, modern equipment, prime location, patents, proprietary technology |
| **Capabilities** | Skilled workforce, efficient production, excellent customer service, strong R&D |
| **Intangible** | Strong brand reputation, loyal customers, good relationships with suppliers, positive company culture |
| **Market position** | Large market share, established distribution network, exclusive contracts |

### Questions to Identify Strengths

- What do we do better than anyone else?
- What unique resources do we have?
- What do our customers praise us for?
- What advantages do we have over competitors?
- What makes us proud?

### Example (Nepali Context)

| Organization | Strength |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dabur Nepal** | Strong brand recognition, wide distribution network across Nepal |
| **A local momo shop** | Secret family recipe, loyal neighborhood customers, low rent |
| **A trekking company** | Experienced guides, excellent safety record, strong online reviews |

---

## Part 2: Weaknesses (Internal Negative)

### What are Weaknesses?

**Weaknesses** are internal limitations, deficiencies, or vulnerabilities that put the organization at a **disadvantage** compared to others. They are things the organization lacks or does poorly.

### Examples of Weaknesses

| Category | Examples |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Resources** | Limited funds, outdated equipment, poor location, lack of patents |
| **Capabilities** | Unskilled workforce, high employee turnover, inefficient processes, weak R&D |
| **Intangible** | Poor brand reputation, bad customer reviews, low employee morale, toxic culture |
| **Market position** | Small market share, weak distribution, over-dependence on few customers |

### Questions to Identify Weaknesses

- What do competitors do better than us?
- What do customers complain about?
- What resources are we lacking?
- Where are we vulnerable?
- What would we avoid doing?

### Example (Nepali Context)

| Organization | Weakness |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dabur Nepal** | Higher prices than local competitors, dependence on Indian parent company |
| **A local momo shop** | No online delivery, small kitchen cannot handle large orders |
| **A trekking company** | Outdated website, no social media presence, poor English communication |

---

## Part 3: Opportunities (External Positive)

### What are Opportunities?

**Opportunities** are external factors, trends, or situations that the organization can **exploit to its advantage**. They represent potential for growth, improvement, or competitive advantage.

### Examples of Opportunities

| Category | Examples |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Market trends** | Growing demand for organic products, increasing health awareness, shift to online shopping |
| **Technology** | New software to automate tasks, social media marketing, AI for customer service |
| **Economic** | Low interest rates, falling raw material prices, growing economy |
| **Political/Legal** | New government incentives, trade agreements, deregulation |
| **Social** | Changing lifestyles, growing middle class, increasing tourism |

### Questions to Identify Opportunities

- What trends are happening in our industry?
- What unmet customer needs exist?
- What new technologies can we use?
- What government programs can we benefit from?
- What gaps have competitors left?

### Example (Nepali Context)

| Organization | Opportunity |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dabur Nepal** | Growing demand for ayurvedic products in India; government's "Make in Nepal" initiative |
| **A local momo shop** | Sign up for Foodmandu/Pathao delivery; offer frozen momos for home cooking |
| **A trekking company** | Post-COVID trekking boom; digital nomads coming to Nepal; promote off-season treks |

---

## Part 4: Threats (External Negative)

### What are Threats?

**Threats** are external factors that could **harm the organization** or create challenges. They are obstacles, risks, or dangers outside the organization's control.

### Examples of Threats

| Category | Examples |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Market trends** | Changing customer preferences, decline in demand, substitute products |
| **Competition** | New competitors entering, aggressive pricing by rivals, competitor innovation |
| **Economic** | Inflation, recession, rising interest rates, currency fluctuation |
| **Political/Legal** | New regulations, tax increases, trade barriers, political instability |
| **Social** | Aging population, migration of skilled workers, negative attitudes toward industry |

### Questions to Identify Threats

- What are competitors doing that could hurt us?
- What new laws or regulations are coming?
- What economic changes could harm us?
- What social changes could reduce demand?
- What risks does our supply chain face?

### Example (Nepali Context)

| Organization | Threat |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dabur Nepal** | Cheap Chinese products entering Nepal; political instability affecting distribution |
| **A local momo shop** | New branded restaurant opening nearby; rising flour and meat prices |
| **A trekking company** | New government trekking fees; climate change affecting trekking seasons |

## Complete SWOT Example: A Nepali Organic Coffee Exporter

### Company Profile
A small Nepali company that grows, roasts, and exports organic coffee to Europe and Japan.

| **STRENGTHS (Internal +)** | **WEAKNESSES (Internal -)** |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1. High-quality, organic certified coffee | 1. Small production volume (cannot fulfill large orders) |
| 2. Unique Himalayan origin story (marketing advantage) | 2. Limited marketing budget |
| 3. Direct relationships with local farmers (fair trade) | 3. No in-house roasting expert |
| 4. Low production costs (Nepali labor) | 4. Poor logistics (shipping delays, high costs) |
| 5. Strong relationships with 2 European buyers | 5. Weak online presence |

| **OPPORTUNITIES (External +)** | **THREATS (External -)** |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1. Global demand for specialty coffee growing 15% yearly | 1. Climate change affecting coffee crop yields |
| 2. New government subsidy for organic exports | 2. Increasing competition from Vietnamese and Ethiopian coffee |
| 3. E-commerce platforms (selling directly to consumers) | 3. Shipping costs rising due to fuel prices |
| 4. Growing coffee culture inside Nepal | 4. Possible import tariff increase in Europe |
| 5. Digital marketing (Instagram, TikTok for brand story) | 5. Political instability in Nepal affecting transport |

## Advantages / Benefits of SWOT Analysis

| Advantage | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Simple and easy to use** | No special training or complex tools required. |
| **Low cost** | Can be done in a meeting room with a whiteboard. |
| **Visual and clear** | Information is organized in an easy-to-understand 2x2 grid. |
| **Combines internal and external** | Gives a complete picture of the organization's situation. |
| **Encourages participation** | Multiple people can contribute (team-based). |
| **Bridges scanning and planning** | Connects environmental scanning to strategy formulation. |
| **Versatile** | Can be used for entire company, a department, a project, or even personal career planning. |

---

## Limitations / Disadvantages of SWOT Analysis

| Limitation | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Can be subjective** | Different people may have different opinions about what is a strength vs. weakness. |
| **No prioritization** | Lists all factors equally, but some are more important than others. |
| **Can become a list, not analysis** | Simply making a list is not enough; you must act on it. |
| **Static snapshot** | SWOT changes over time; today's strength may be tomorrow's weakness. |
| **Can be superficial** | May miss deep, root-cause issues if not done carefully. |
| **May lead to oversimplification** | Real business situations are more complex than four boxes. |

> **How to overcome limitations:** Use SWOT as a starting point, not an ending point. Prioritize factors. Update regularly. Combine with other tools (PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces).

## Personal SWOT Analysis (Bonus for Your Own Career)

SWOT is not just for businesses. You can use it for yourself.

| **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Good at studying management concepts | Poor at math/accounting |
| Fluent in English and Nepali | Weak presentation skills |
| Hardworking and punctual | Procrastinates on long assignments |

| **Opportunities** | **Threats** |
| :--- | :--- |
| Internship programs at local companies | High competition for good jobs |
| Online courses to learn new skills | Economic slowdown reducing hiring |
| Networking through college events | AI replacing entry-level office jobs |

**Strategies:**
- **SO:** Use hardworking nature + internship opportunities → Apply for 5 internships this semester.
- **WO:** Improve presentation skills + online courses → Take a free public speaking course on YouTube.
- **ST:** Use English fluency + high competition → Get an IELTS/TOEFL certification to stand out.
- **WT:** Reduce procrastination + AI threat → Learn AI tools (ChatGPT, Excel automation) to stay relevant.

## Sample Exam Question & Answer

**Q:** What is SWOT analysis? Explain its components with examples. How can it be used for strategic planning?

**A:**

**Definition:** SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool that identifies and evaluates an organization's internal Strengths and Weaknesses and external Opportunities and Threats.

**Components of SWOT:**

| Component | Environment | Meaning | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strengths** | Internal (Positive) | What the organization does well or unique resources it has | Strong brand, skilled workforce, patents |
| **Weaknesses** | Internal (Negative) | Limitations or deficiencies that put the organization at a disadvantage | Outdated technology, poor location, high costs |
| **Opportunities** | External (Positive) | External trends or situations that can be exploited for growth | Growing market, new technology, government incentives |
| **Threats** | External (Negative) | External obstacles or dangers that could harm the organization | New competitors, economic recession, new laws |


**Example:** A small organic coffee exporter in Nepal identifies:
- Strength: High-quality organic certification
- Opportunity: Growing global demand for specialty coffee
- SO Strategy: Increase marketing to existing European buyers to expand sales.

**Conclusion:** SWOT analysis is a simple, powerful tool that bridges environmental scanning and strategic planning. While it has limitations (subjectivity, lack of prioritization), it remains one of the most widely used tools in management.
