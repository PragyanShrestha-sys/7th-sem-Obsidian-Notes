## [[Concept of Planning and Decision Making]]

**Concept of Planning:** Planning is the primary function of management. It involves setting organizational goals, developing strategies, and determining the actions and resources needed to achieve those goals. It is forward-looking, continuous, and pervasive. The planning process includes steps like setting objectives, developing premises, identifying alternatives, evaluating them, selecting the best, and implementing.

**Concept of Decision Making:** Decision making is the process of choosing one course of action from two or more alternatives. It is the heart of planning and management. Decisions can be programmed (routine) or non-programmed (unique), and made under certainty, risk, or uncertainty. The rational decision-making process involves identifying the problem, generating alternatives, evaluating them, and selecting the best.

## [[Decision Making defination and approaches]]

| S.No. | Approach                         | Key Idea                                       | Type                                     |
| :---- | :------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------- |
| 1     | **Rational/Economic Approach**   | Managers make logical, optimal decisions.      | Prescriptive (how they *should* decide)  |
| 2     | **Bounded Rationality Approach** | Managers are limited, so they "satisfice."     | Descriptive (how they *actually* decide) |
| 3     | **Intuitive Approach**           | Decisions based on gut feeling and experience. | Descriptive                              |
| 4     | **Creative Approach**            | Decisions based on novel, innovative ideas.    | Prescriptive                             |

## [[Problem solving -Concepts, Types and Strategies]]

**Types of Problems: WICOS** (sounds like "wicked")

- **W** = Well-structured
- **I** = Ill-structured
- **C** = Crisis
- **O** = Operational
- **S** = Strategic

**Strategies: A H T B R D M A** (sounds like "A Hot Burger D M A")

- **A** = Algorithm
- **H** = Heuristic
- **T** = Trial and Error
- **B** = Brainstorming
- **R** = Root Cause Analysis
- **D** = Divide and Conquer
- **M** = Means-Ends Analysis
- **A** = Analogical Reasoning
