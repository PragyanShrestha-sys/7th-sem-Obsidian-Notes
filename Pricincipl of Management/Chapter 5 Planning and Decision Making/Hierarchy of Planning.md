
## What is the Hierarchy of Planning? (Definition)

The **Hierarchy of Planning** refers to the **nested, logical arrangement** of plans within an organization, ranging from the **broadest, most abstract** (vision/mission) down to the **most specific, detailed** (rules, schedules, budgets). Each level of the hierarchy supports and guides the level below it.

> **In simple words:** It is a pyramid of plans where the top plan gives direction to the middle plan, which gives direction to the bottom plan. Nothing at the bottom should contradict anything at the top.

---

## Why is the Hierarchy Important?

| Reason | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Alignment** | Ensures daily actions support long-term goals. |
| **Consistency** | Prevents contradictions (e.g., strategy says "quality focus" but operational plan says "produce fast and cheap"). |
| **Clarity** | Each level knows what it must achieve to support the level above. |
| **Efficiency** | Resources are allocated logically from top goals down to bottom tasks. |
| **Accountability** | Each level can be evaluated on how well it supports the level above. |

## Detailed Explanation of Each Level in the Hierarchy

### Level 1: Vision (The Dream)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | A mental image of what the organization wants to become in the future. |
| **Time horizon** | Very long-term (10–20+ years) |
| **Nature** | Inspiring, aspirational, emotional |
| **Example** | "A world without hunger." (NGO) / "To be the most trusted brand in Nepal." |

### Level 2: Mission (The Purpose)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | States why the organization exists, who its customers are, and what it does. |
| **Time horizon** | Long-term (5–10 years) |
| **Nature** | Clear, action-oriented, practical |
| **Example** | "To provide affordable, high-quality mobile phones to every Nepali household." |

### Level 3: Objectives (The Goals)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Specific, measurable targets derived from the mission. |
| **Time horizon** | Medium to long-term (1–5 years) |
| **Nature** | SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) |
| **Example** | "Achieve 20% market share in Nepal by 2027." |

### Level 4: Strategies (The Broad Action Plans)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Long-term action plans to achieve objectives. |
| **Nature** | Broad, directional, resource-intensive |
| **Example** | "Enter the rural market by partnering with 1,000 local kirana stores." |

### Level 5: Policies (The Guidelines)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | General guidelines that shape decisions and actions. |
| **Nature** | Flexible, broad, allows managerial discretion |
| **Example** | "We promote from within whenever possible." / "30-day return policy." |

### Level 6: Procedures (The Step-by-Step Methods)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Prescribed sequence of steps for routine activities. |
| **Nature** | More rigid than policies, less rigid than rules |
| **Example** | "Step 1: Receive customer complaint. Step 2: Log in system. Step 3: Assign to agent. Step 4: Resolve within 48 hours." |

### Level 7: Rules (The Rigid Directives)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Specific, rigid commands that must be followed exactly; no discretion. |
| **Nature** | Most rigid, zero flexibility |
| **Example** | "No smoking in the office." / "Report by 9:00 AM sharp." |

### Level 8: Programs (The Single-Use Projects)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | A complex set of activities designed to accomplish a specific, non-recurring goal. |
| **Nature** | Single-use, time-bound, cross-functional |
| **Example** | "Program to launch a new product next quarter." |

### Level 9: Budgets (The Numerical Plans)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | A plan expressed in numerical terms (money, units, time). |
| **Nature** | Quantitative, controlling mechanism |
| **Example** | "Marketing budget: Rs. 50 lakh for Q3." / "Production budget: 10,000 units per month." |

### Level 10: Schedules (The Timelines)

| Aspect | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | A plan that shows when specific tasks must be completed. |
| **Nature** | Time-specific, detailed, operational |
| **Example** | "Week 1: Order raw materials. Week 2: Begin production. Week 3: Quality check. Week 4: Ship to stores." |

## Simplified Hierarchy (Exam-Friendly Version)

Many textbooks combine the hierarchy into **four or five main levels**. Here is the simplified version:

```
         ┌─────────────────────────────┐
         │    Strategic Plans (Top)     │  ← Vision, Mission, Objectives, Strategies
         │    (Broad, long-term)        │
         └─────────────┬───────────────┘
                       │
         ┌─────────────▼───────────────┐
         │   Standing Plans (Middle)    │  ← Policies, Procedures, Rules
         │   (For repeated use)         │
         └─────────────┬───────────────┘
                       │
         ┌─────────────▼───────────────┐
         │   Single-Use Plans (Lower)   │  ← Programs, Budgets, Schedules
         │   (For one-time events)      │
         └─────────────────────────────┘
```

Or even simpler:

| Level | Type of Plans | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Top (Direction)** | Vision, Mission, Objectives, Strategies | Why? What? How (broadly)? |
| **Middle (Guidelines)** | Policies, Procedures, Rules | What are the rules of the game? |
| **Bottom (Action)** | Programs, Budgets, Schedules | Exactly when, who, how much? |

## How the Hierarchy Works: A Complete Example

**Company:** A mobile phone retailer in Nepal.

| Hierarchy Level | Plan | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vision** | Dream | "To connect every Nepali household to the digital world." |
| **Mission** | Purpose | "To provide affordable, reliable smartphones and after-sales service across Nepal." |
| **Objectives** | SMART goals | "Achieve 15% market share in Nepal within 3 years." |
| **Strategies** | Broad action | "Expand to 50 new retail outlets in semi-urban areas within 2 years." |
| **Policies** | Guidelines | "All products come with a 1-year warranty." / "We do not sell refurbished phones as new." |
| **Procedures** | Step-by-step | "Step 1: Customer selects phone. Step 2: Scan barcode. Step 3: Collect payment. Step 4: Register warranty online." |
| **Rules** | Rigid directives | "No refunds after 7 days." / "Store opens at 10 AM sharp." |
| **Programs** | Single-use project | "Festival season discount program (Dashain offer): 10% off on all models." |
| **Budgets** | Numerical plans | "Marketing budget: Rs. 20 lakh. Inventory budget: Rs. 1 crore." |
| **Schedules** | Timelines | "Week 1: Order stock. Week 2: Train staff. Week 3: Launch advertisement. Week 4: Start sales." |

## The Principle of Alignment (Very Important for Exams)

**Lower-level plans must not contradict higher-level plans.**

| If higher level says... | Then lower level CANNOT say... |
| :--- | :--- |
| Mission: "Quality products" | Operational plan: "Cut quality checks to save time" |
| Policy: "Customer first" | Rule: "No refunds under any circumstance" |
| Strategy: "Expand to rural areas" | Budget: "Zero allocation for rural transport" |

> **Good alignment example:** Strategy = "Low-cost leader" → Policy = "Minimize overhead" → Rule = "All travel requires approval" → Budget = "Minimal travel expenses."

## Sample Exam Question & Answer

**Q:** Explain the hierarchy of planning in an organization. Why is it important?

**A:**

**Definition:** The hierarchy of planning is the logical arrangement of plans from the most broad and long-term at the top to the most specific and short-term at the bottom. Each level guides and supports the level below it.

**The Hierarchy (from top to bottom):**

1.  **Vision:** The aspirational dream of what the organization wants to become. (Example: "To be the most trusted brand in Nepal.")

2.  **Mission:** The purpose of the organization—why it exists and what it does. (Example: "To provide high-quality, affordable consumer goods.")

3.  **Objectives:** Specific, measurable goals derived from the mission. (Example: "Achieve 20% market share by 2027.")

4.  **Strategies:** Broad action plans to achieve objectives. (Example: "Expand distribution to rural areas.")

5.  **Policies:** General guidelines for decision making. (Example: "30-day return policy.")

6.  **Procedures:** Step-by-step methods for routine tasks. (Example: "Steps to process a customer complaint.")

7.  **Rules:** Rigid directives with no discretion. (Example: "No smoking in the office.")

8.  **Programs:** Single-use plans for specific projects. (Example: "Festival discount program.")

9.  **Budgets:** Plans expressed in numerical terms. (Example: "Marketing budget: Rs. 10 lakh.")

10. **Schedules:** Timelines for completing tasks. (Example: "Launch date: Nov 15.")

**Importance of the Hierarchy:**

1.  **Ensures alignment:** Daily actions support long-term goals.
2.  **Prevents contradictions:** Lower plans cannot violate higher plans.
3.  **Provides clarity:** Everyone knows how their work fits the big picture.
4.  **Improves accountability:** Each level can be evaluated on how well it supports the level above.
5.  **Efficient resource allocation:** Resources flow logically from top priorities to bottom tasks.

**Conclusion:** The hierarchy of planning is the backbone of effective management. Without it, an organization's plans become disconnected, contradictory, and ineffective.
