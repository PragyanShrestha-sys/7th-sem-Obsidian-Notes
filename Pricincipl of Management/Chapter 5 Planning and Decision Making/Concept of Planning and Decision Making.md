### What is Planning? (Definition)

**Planning** is the process of **setting goals**, **developing strategies**, and **determining the tasks and resources** needed to achieve those goals. It answers the question: *"Where are we now, where do we want to go, and how will we get there?"*

> **In simple words:** Planning is deciding *in advance* what to do, how to do it, when to do it, and who will do it.

### Types of Plans (Quick Overview)

| Basis           | Type of Plan                                                            | Explanation     |
| :-------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------- |
| **Time period** | Strategic (long-term), Tactical (medium-term), Operational (short-term) | How far ahead?  |
| **Use**         | Single-use (for one project) vs. Standing (repeated use, like policies) | How often used? |
| **Scope**       | Corporate, Business, Functional plans                                   | Which level?    |

### Steps in the Planning Process (Very Important for Exams)

1.  **Setting objectives** – Define what you want to achieve.
2.  **Developing premises** – Make assumptions about future conditions (economic, political, etc.).
3.  **Identifying alternatives** – List different ways to reach the goal.
4.  **Evaluating alternatives** – Compare pros, cons, costs, risks of each.
5.  **Selecting the best alternative** – This is **decision making**.
6.  **Formulating derivative plans** – Create sub-plans (budget, schedule, resources).
7.  **Implementing the plan** – Put the plan into action.
8.  **Reviewing and controlling** – Check progress and make corrections.

## Part 2: Concept of Decision Making

### What is Decision Making? (Definition)

**Decision making** is the process of **choosing one course of action from two or more alternatives**. It is the core of planning and managmente.

> **In simple words:** Decision making is selecting the best option when you have choices.

### Key Definitions (For Exams)

| Scholar | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Peter Drucker** | "Whatever a manager does, he does through decision making." |
| **Herbert Simon** | "Decision making is the process of selecting a course of action from among alternatives. It is the heart of administration." |
| **Simple Definition** | Decision making = problem identification + alternative evaluation + choice. |

### Types of Decision Making (Key Distinction)

| Basis         | Type               | Explanation                                                   | Example                                                    |
| :------------ | :----------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Frequency** | **Programmed**     | Routine, repetitive decisions with clear rules.               | Reordering office supplies when stock is low.              |
|               | **Non-programmed** | Unique, complex, novel decisions with no ready-made solution. | Launching a new product or entering a new market.          |
| **Certainty** | **Certainty**      | Full knowledge of outcomes.                                   | Buying raw materials at a fixed price.                     |
|               | **Risk**           | Probable outcomes known (based on data).                      | Investing in a new machine with known success probability. |
|               | **Uncertainty**    | Outcomes unknown; no data available.                          | Entering a completely new foreign market.                  |
| **Level**     | **Strategic**      | Top management, long-term, high impact.                       | Merging with another company.                              |
|               | **Tactical**       | Middle management, medium-term.                               | Deciding a quarterly budget.                               |
|               | **Operational**    | Lower management, daily decisions.                            | Scheduling employee shifts.                                |

### Steps in the Decision Making Process (Rational Model)

1.  **Identify the problem** – Recognize that a decision is needed.
2.  **Diagnose the problem** – Find the root cause.
3.  **Establish decision criteria** – Define what a "good" decision looks like (cost, time, quality, etc.).
4.  **Generate alternatives** – List possible solutions.
5.  **Evaluate alternatives** – Compare each against the criteria.
6.  **Select the best alternative** – Make the choice.
7.  **Implement the decision** – Put it into action.
8.  **Evaluate the decision** – Did it solve the problem? If not, adjust.

## The Relationship Between Planning and Decision Making (Very Important)

Many students get confused. Here is the clear relationship:

| | **Planning** | **Decision Making** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scope** | Broader. Includes setting goals and deciding how to achieve them. | Narrower. The specific act of choosing among alternatives. |
| **Role** | The primary function of management. | The core/heart of planning. |
| **Sequence** | Planning *contains* decision making as one of its steps. | Decision making occurs within the planning process. |
| **Without the other** | Planning without decision making is impossible (you must choose). | Decision making without planning has no direction. |

> **Simple formula:** Planning = Objectives + Decision Making + Action Steps

### Diagram for Understanding

```
PLANNING (The umbrella function)
│
├── 1. Set Objectives
├── 2. Make Assumptions (Premises)
├── 3. List Alternatives
├── 4. EVALUATE ALTERNATIVES ──────┐
├── 5. MAKE A CHOICE  ←─────────────┘  (This is DECISION MAKING)
├── 6. Create Sub-plans
└── 7. Implement & Control
```

| :--- | :--- | :--- |

## Real-World Example (To Connect Both)

**Scenario:** A small restaurant manager.

| Activity | Is it Planning? | Is it Decision Making? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Setting a goal: "Increase monthly profit by 10%" | ✅ Yes (objective setting) | ❌ No choice yet |
| Listing options: (a) raise prices, (b) reduce costs, (c) run an offer | ✅ Yes (identifying alternatives) | ❌ Still exploring |
| Choosing option (b) "reduce costs" from the list | ✅ Part of planning | ✅ **This is decision making** |
| Creating a budget to reduce food waste | ✅ Yes (derivative plan) | ❌ No |
| Deciding to buy from a cheaper supplier | ✅ Part of planning | ✅ **This is decision making** |

> **Conclusion:** Decision making is the critical *choice* step inside the larger *planning* process.

## Sample Exam Question & Answer

**Q:** Explain the concept of planning and decision making. How are they related?

**A:**

**Concept of Planning:** Planning is the primary function of management. It involves setting organizational goals, developing strategies, and determining the actions and resources needed to achieve those goals. It is forward-looking, continuous, and pervasive. The planning process includes steps like setting objectives, developing premises, identifying alternatives, evaluating them, selecting the best, and implementing.

**Concept of Decision Making:** Decision making is the process of choosing one course of action from two or more alternatives. It is the heart of planning and management. Decisions can be programmed (routine) or non-programmed (unique), and made under certainty, risk, or uncertainty. The rational decision-making process involves identifying the problem, generating alternatives, evaluating them, and selecting the best.

**Relationship between Planning and Decision Making:**

1.  **Decision making is a step within planning:** In the planning process, after identifying alternatives, managers must evaluate and choose one. That choice is decision making.
2.  **Planning without decision making is impossible:** If a manager does not make a choice, no plan can be finalized.
3.  **Decision making without planning is directionless:** Decisions need a context. Planning provides the goals and criteria that guide which choice is "best."
4.  **Planning gives decision making its purpose:** The quality of a decision is measured by how well it helps achieve the plan's objectives.

**Conclusion:** Planning is the broader framework, and decision making is the critical act of choice that makes planning operational. Both are essential for effective management.
