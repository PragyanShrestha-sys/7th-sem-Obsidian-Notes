Excellent. You've now covered the **components** of strategic planning (vision, mission, SWOT, etc.). The next logical step is understanding how those components are **put into action**—specifically, the two critical phases of **Formulation** (creating the strategy) and **Implementation** (executing the strategy).

This is a very common exam topic because it highlights the difference between **planning** and **doing**.

Here is a comprehensive, exam-focused explanation of the **Formulation and Implementation of Strategic Plans**.

## Part 1: Formulation of Strategic Plans

### What is Strategy Formulation? (Definition)

**Strategy Formulation** is the process of **developing** or **creating** a strategic plan. It involves analyzing the environment, setting objectives, generating strategic alternatives, and choosing the best course of action.

> **In simple words:** Strategy formulation answers the question: *"What should we do?"*

### Key Characteristics of Strategy Formulation

| Characteristic | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Intellectual process** | Requires thinking, analysis, creativity, and judgment. |
| **Top management driven** | Primarily done by CEO, board, and senior executives. |
| **Long-term focus** | Looks at the big picture (3–10+ years). |
| **Conceptual** | Produces ideas, plans, and documents. |
| **Precedes implementation** | Happens before any action is taken. |
| **Resource planning** | Decides what resources will be needed. |

---

### The Strategy Formulation Process (8 Steps)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                STRATEGY FORMULATION PROCESS                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│  Step 1:  Develop Vision & Mission (Why do we exist?)           │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 2:  Conduct Environmental Scanning (SWOT, PESTEL)         │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 3:  Set Strategic Objectives (SMART goals)                │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 4:  Identify Strategic Alternatives (Options)             │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 5:  Evaluate Alternatives (Pros, cons, costs, risks)      │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 6:  Select the Best Strategy (Decision making)            │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 7:  Develop Derivative Plans (Functional, tactical)       │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Step 8:  Prepare Formal Strategic Plan (Document)              │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Detailed Explanation of Each Step

| Step | What it means | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Vision & Mission** | Define purpose and aspiration. | Vision: "To connect every Nepali to the digital economy." Mission: "To provide affordable mobile internet services." |
| **2. Environmental Scanning** | Analyze internal and external environment. | SWOT shows: Strength = existing towers; Threat = new competitor. |
| **3. Strategic Objectives** | Set specific long-term goals. | "Achieve 40% market share in Nepal within 5 years." |
| **4. Strategic Alternatives** | List possible strategies. | Options: (a) Lower prices, (b) Improve network quality, (c) Bundle with devices. |
| **5. Evaluate Alternatives** | Compare each option. | Lower prices = higher volume but lower profit margin. Better quality = higher investment. |
| **6. Select Best Strategy** | Choose the best option. | "We will differentiate on network quality and charge a premium price." |
| **7. Derivative Plans** | Create supporting plans. | Marketing plan, HR plan (hire engineers), finance plan (raise Rs. 200 crore). |
| **8. Formal Strategic Plan** | Document everything. | A written document approved by the board. |

---

### Types of Strategies Formulated (Three Levels)

| Level | What is formulated | Who does it | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Corporate Strategy** | What businesses to be in? Enter/exit industries? Allocate resources across divisions? | CEO, Board | "We will exit the textile business and focus only on IT services." |
| **Business Strategy** | How to compete in each market? Cost leadership? Differentiation? Focus? | Division heads | "Our mobile division will compete on cost leadership in rural areas." |
| **Functional Strategy** | How to support business strategy? Marketing, finance, HR, operations plans. | Department managers | "Marketing: Use local influencers. HR: Hire 50 sales staff in 6 months." |

---

### Generic Strategies (Michael Porter) – Formulated at Business Level

| Strategy | What it means | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cost Leadership** | Be the lowest-cost producer in the industry. | Walmart, Bikalpa (Nepali discount store) |
| **Differentiation** | Offer unique, superior products that customers pay more for. | Apple, Starbucks, Himalayan Java Coffee |
| **Focus (Cost)** | Target a niche market with low-cost products. | A small store selling only budget items for students |
| **Focus (Differentiation)** | Target a niche market with unique, premium products. | A luxury trekking company for wealthy tourists |

> **Warning:** Trying to do all strategies at once leads to being "stuck in the middle" (failing at all).

## Part 2: Implementation of Strategic Plans

### What is Strategy Implementation? (Definition)

**Strategy Implementation** is the process of **putting the strategic plan into action**. It involves allocating resources, changing organizational structure, motivating people, and monitoring progress to achieve the strategic objectives.

> **In simple words:** Strategy implementation answers the question: *"How do we make it happen?"*

### Key Characteristics of Strategy Implementation

| Characteristic | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Action-oriented** | Requires doing, not just thinking. |
| **Involves all levels** | Everyone in the organization plays a role. |
| **Short to medium-term focus** | Day-to-day, monthly, quarterly actions. |
| **Operational** | Deals with budgets, schedules, people, tasks. |
| **Follows formulation** | Happens after the strategy is chosen. |
| **More difficult than formulation** | Execution is harder than planning (common reason for failure). |

---

### The Strategy Implementation Process (7 Key Elements)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│               STRATEGY IMPLEMENTATION PROCESS                    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│  Element 1: Develop an Implementation Plan (Who does what?)     │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 2: Allocate Resources (Budget, people, equipment)      │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 3: Align Organizational Structure (Create/change depts)│
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 4: Communicate the Strategy (Explain to all employees) │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 5: Motivate and Lead People (Inspire action)           │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 6: Develop Action Plans (Tactical & operational)       │
│           ↓                                                      │
│  Element 7: Monitor, Control, and Adjust (Review progress)      │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Detailed Explanation of Each Element

| Element | What it means | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Implementation Plan** | A detailed plan assigning tasks, responsibilities, and deadlines. | "Marketing Manager will launch ads by March 1. HR Manager will hire 10 staff by April 15." |
| **2. Allocate Resources** | Distribute budget, people, equipment to the right places. | "Rs. 5 crore allocated to marketing; Rs. 10 crore to new machinery." |
| **3. Align Structure** | Change organizational structure to support strategy. | Create a new "Digital Division" if strategy is digital transformation. |
| **4. Communicate Strategy** | Tell all employees what the strategy is and why it matters. | Town hall meeting, emails, posters, training sessions. |
| **5. Motivate and Lead** | Inspire people to work toward strategic goals. | Bonuses for meeting targets, recognition, leadership by example. |
| **6. Action Plans** | Break strategy into daily/weekly tasks. | "Sales team will visit 10 new retailers per day." |
| **7. Monitor and Control** | Track progress, compare actual vs. planned, take corrective action. | Monthly review meetings; if behind schedule, add resources or change tactics. |

### The 7S Framework (McKinsey) – A Holistic View of Implementation

McKinsey's 7S model shows that **all seven elements** must be aligned for successful strategy implementation.

```
                    ┌─────────────┐
                    │   STRATEGY  │
                    └──────┬──────┘
                           │
         ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
         │                 │                 │
         ▼                 ▼                 ▼
   ┌─────────┐       ┌─────────┐       ┌─────────┐
   │STRUCTURE│       │ SYSTEMS │       │ STYLE   │
   └─────────┘       └─────────┘       └─────────┘
         │                 │                 │
         └─────────────────┼─────────────────┘
                           │
         ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
         │                 │                 │
         ▼                 ▼                 ▼
   ┌─────────┐       ┌─────────┐       ┌─────────┐
   │ STAFF   │       │ SKILLS  │       │SHARED   │
   │         │       │         │       │ VALUES  │
   └─────────┘       └─────────┘       └─────────┘
```

| Element | Meaning | Implementation Question |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strategy** | The plan itself | Is the plan clear and well-understood? |
| **Structure** | Organizational hierarchy | Does our structure support the strategy? |
| **Systems** | Processes, procedures, IT | Do our systems enable execution? |
| **Style** | Leadership and culture | Does leadership model the right behavior? |
| **Staff** | People and their capabilities | Do we have the right people? |
| **Skills** | Organizational competencies | Do we have the necessary skills? |
| **Shared Values** | Core beliefs and culture | Does everyone believe in the strategy? |

> **Key insight:** Changing strategy without changing structure, systems, skills, or culture will lead to failure.

## Key Differences: Formulation vs. Implementation (Exam-Ready)

| Basis | Strategy Formulation | Strategy Implementation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Meaning** | Developing the strategy (planning) | Executing the strategy (doing) |
| **Focus** | "What to do?" | "How to make it happen?" |
| **Time horizon** | Long-term (3–10+ years) | Short to medium-term (daily to 2 years) |
| **Who does it** | Top management primarily | All levels (top, middle, lower) |
| **Nature** | Intellectual, analytical, conceptual | Operational, action-oriented, practical |
| **Output** | Strategic plan (document) | Results, outcomes, changed organization |
| **Difficulty** | Less difficult | More difficult (most failures happen here) |
| **Resources** | Planning resources (research, meetings) | Actual resources (money, people, time) |
| **Risk** | Risk of wrong direction | Risk of poor execution |
| **Key question** | "Is this the right strategy?" | "Are we executing it correctly?" |

## Common Barriers to Strategy Implementation (Why Plans Fail)

| Barrier | Explanation | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Poor communication** | Employees don't understand the strategy. | "I didn't know we were supposed to focus on customer service." |
| **Lack of resources** | Not enough budget, people, or time allocated. | The plan requires 10 new staff, but only 3 are hired. |
| **Resistance to change** | Employees resist new ways of working. | "We've always done it this way. Why change?" |
| **Weak leadership** | Managers don't commit to or model the strategy. | CEO says "cut costs" but buys a new luxury car. |
| **Inadequate structure** | Organization structure does not support the strategy. | Strategy needs faster decisions, but hierarchy has 7 approval layers. |
| **Poor monitoring** | No system to track progress. | Nobody knows if we are on track until it's too late. |
| **Unrealistic timeline** | The plan expects too much too quickly. | "Achieve 50% market share in 3 months" is impossible. |

## Factors for Successful Strategy Implementation

| Success Factor | Explanation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Clear communication** | Every employee understands the strategy and their role. |
| **Leadership commitment** | Top management actively supports and models the strategy. |
| **Adequate resources** | Sufficient budget, people, time, and equipment are provided. |
| **Aligned structure** | Organizational structure enables (not blocks) execution. |
| **Employee involvement** | People are involved in planning and feel ownership. |
| **Effective monitoring** | Regular reviews and corrective action. |
| **Flexibility** | Ability to adjust the plan when conditions change. |
| **Rewards and recognition** | Incentives for meeting strategic goals. |

## Complete Example: Formulation and Implementation

**Company:** A Nepali dairy company (example: Nepal Dairy)

### Formulation Phase

| Step | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Vision** | "To be the most trusted dairy brand in Nepal." |
| **Mission** | "To provide fresh, pure, and affordable milk products to every Nepali household." |
| **SWOT** | S: Strong distribution; W: Old machinery; O: Growing demand for packaged milk; T: New competitor from India |
| **Objective** | "Increase market share from 15% to 25% within 3 years." |
| **Strategic Alternative** | Option A: Lower prices; Option B: Improve quality and launch premium products; Option C: Expand to new districts |
| **Chosen Strategy** | "Differentiate on quality and freshness. Launch 'Farm Fresh' premium milk line. Expand to 10 new districts." |

### Implementation Phase

| Element | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Implementation Plan** | Marketing: Launch "Farm Fresh" brand by March. Operations: Upgrade machinery by Feb. HR: Hire 50 new delivery staff. |
| **Resources** | Budget: Rs. 15 crore allocated. New machinery ordered. 50 new hires approved. |
| **Structure** | Create new "Premium Products Division" with its own manager. |
| **Communication** | Town hall meeting to explain strategy. Weekly emails with updates. |
| **Motivation** | Bonuses for meeting market share targets. "Employee of the month" for innovation. |
| **Action Plans** | Daily: Production targets. Weekly: Distribution routes. Monthly: Market share review. |
| **Monitoring** | Monthly review meeting. Quarterly strategy refresh. If market share grows too slowly, increase marketing budget. |

## Sample Exam Question & Answer

**Q:** Distinguish between strategy formulation and strategy implementation. Explain the key steps in each.

**A:**

**Definition:**
- **Strategy Formulation** is the process of developing the strategic plan—analyzing the environment, setting objectives, generating alternatives, and choosing the best strategy.
- **Strategy Implementation** is the process of executing the strategic plan—allocating resources, aligning structure, leading people, and monitoring progress.

**Key Differences:**

| Basis | Formulation | Implementation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Meaning | Developing the strategy | Executing the strategy |
| Focus | "What to do?" | "How to make it happen?" |
| Who does it | Top management primarily | All levels |
| Nature | Intellectual, analytical | Action-oriented, operational |
| Difficulty | Less difficult | More difficult |

**Steps in Strategy Formulation:**

1. Develop vision and mission
2. Conduct environmental scanning (SWOT, PESTEL)
3. Set strategic objectives (SMART goals)
4. Identify strategic alternatives
5. Evaluate alternatives (pros, cons, costs, risks)
6. Select the best strategy (decision making)
7. Develop derivative plans (functional, tactical)
8. Prepare formal strategic plan document

**Steps in Strategy Implementation:**

1. Develop an implementation plan (who does what by when)
2. Allocate resources (budget, people, equipment)
3. Align organizational structure (create/change departments)
4. Communicate the strategy to all employees
5. Motivate and lead people (incentives, inspiration)
6. Develop action plans (tactical, operational, daily tasks)
7. Monitor, control, and adjust (reviews, corrective action)

**Conclusion:** Formulation without implementation is useless. Implementation without formulation is directionless. Both are essential for strategic success. However, most organizations fail not because they chose the wrong strategy, but because they executed poorly.
