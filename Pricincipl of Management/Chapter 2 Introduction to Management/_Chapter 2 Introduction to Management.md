## [[Principles of Management]]

**Principles of management** are the fundamental, universal truths and guidelines that serve as the foundation for managerial decision-making and action. They are not strict, rigid laws (like in physics) but rather **flexible guidelines** that can be adapted to different situations.

---
## [[Process and Functions of Management]]


> **Key insight:** The **functions** are the building blocks. The **process** is the dynamic flow of those functions over time. In practice, they are two ways of describing the same reality.


The **management process** is the continuous series of actions or operations conducted by managers to achieve organizational goals. It is often depicted as a circular or iterative flow:

```
         Planning
            ↑
    Controlling → Organizing
            ↓
         Leading and decision making
```

## [[Managerial Skills and Roles]]

### Managerial Skills (Katz)
1. **Technical** – Job-specific expertise. Most important for first-line managers.
2. **Human** – Working with people. Equally important at all levels.
3. **Conceptual** – Big-picture thinking. Most important for top managers.

### Managerial Roles (Mintzberg)
- **3 Interpersonal** – Figurehead, Leader, Liaison
- **3 Informational** – Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson
- **4 Decisional** – Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator

### Core Relationship
- **Skills** = Capabilities (what you can do)
- **Roles** = Behaviors (what you actually do)
- Effective managers develop all three skills and perform all ten roles, but **the mix changes by level and situation**.
