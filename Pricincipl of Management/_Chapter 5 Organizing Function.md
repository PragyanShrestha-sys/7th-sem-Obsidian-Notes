## [[Introduction and Principles ]]
Organizing is the process of identifying and grouping the work to be done, defining and delegating authority and responsibility, and establishing relationships so that people can work together effectively to achieve the organization's goals.

## [[Departmentalization Meaning and Types]]

**In simple words:** Departmentalization is the process of dividing an organization into different departments or units, where each department handles a specific set of related activities.

## [[Deleation of Authority]]

elegation is the process by which a manager assigns a portion of their own workload to a subordinate, along with sufficient **authority** (power/rights) to complete that task, while the subordinate accepts **responsibility** and becomes **accountable** to the manager for the results.

> **1. Authority** (Right to act) → **GIVEN** to subordinate.
> **2. Responsibility** (Duty to perform) → **ACCEPTED** by subordinate.
> **3. Accountability** (Answerability) → **REMAINS** with the superior. (It flows UPWARD).

## [[Centralization and Decentralization]]

Centralization refers to a system where the authority to make major decisions is concentrated in the hands of a **few top-level managers** (usually the CEO, Board of Directors, or Head Office). Middle and lower-level managers simply *execute* the orders passed down to them; they have little to no freedom to make independent choices.

Decentralization is the systematic (and deliberate) delegation of authority to **all levels of management**—right down to the lower-level supervisors and branch offices. It is NOT just "delegation"; it is the *philosophy* that decision-making power should be pushed as far down the hierarchy as possible.

## [[Concept of Organic and mechanist view of organization]]

A Mechanistic organization is a rigid, hierarchical, and highly formalized structure. It operates like a well-oiled **machine**. Every person has a specific, fixed job, and rules dictate exactly how they must do it. Communication strictly follows the official chain of command (Scalar Chain). 

**When is it used?** 
It is best suited for **stable, predictable, and slowly changing environments** (e.g., government agencies, postal services, mass manufacturing, traditional banks).

An Organic organization is a flexible, fluid, and highly adaptive structure. It operates like a **living organism**—it grows, shrinks, and changes its shape to survive in its environment. There are fewer rules, and job descriptions are constantly evolving. People communicate across departments freely to solve problems.

**When is it used?** 
It is best suited for **dynamic, uncertain, and rapidly changing environments** (e.g., Tech startups, AI companies, creative advertising agencies, COVID-era rapid-response teams).

| :-------------------- | :---------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------- | :--------------------------------- |
