## [[Introduction to Evolution of management]]

**Evolution of management thought** refers to the gradual development and change in management theories, principles, and practices over time – from pre-industrial times to the present day. It traces how our understanding of organizations, workers, and managerial roles has progressed in response to changing economic, social, technological, and political conditions.

## [[Human Relation and behavioural science theories]]

## Part 1: Human Relations Theory (1930s–1950s)

A school of thought that emphasizes the **social and psychological needs** of workers, arguing that satisfied workers are more productive. It focuses on **informal groups**, **communication**, and **leadership** rather than just rules and money.
## Part 2: Behavioural Science Theory (1950s–present)

A more **scientific and rigorous** extension of Human Relations. It applies psychology, sociology, anthropology, and economics to understand **individual and group behavior** in organizations. It moves beyond "happy workers are productive" to "what specifically motivates, satisfies, and influences people."
