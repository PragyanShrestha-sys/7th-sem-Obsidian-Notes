
Managers analyze the environment using this classic split:
#### 1. Internal Environment (Factors Inside the Business)
These are things the business **can control or influence** directly.
- **Examples:** Company culture, employees, management structure, internal policies, technology owned, financial resources.
- **Management role:** Improve, align, and strengthen these to face external challenges.

#### 2. External Environment (Factors Outside the Business)
These are things the business **cannot control**, but must adapt to. This is further divided into:

| **Micro (Task) Environment** (Close, direct impact) | **Macro (General) Environment** (Broad, indirect impact) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Customers | Economic factors (inflation, GDP, income) |
| Suppliers | Political & Legal factors (laws, stability, taxes) |
| Competitors | Social & Cultural factors (demographics, lifestyles, values) |
| Intermediaries (distributors, retailers) | Technological factors (automation, AI, digital tools) |
| Publics (media, local community) | Ecological/Natural factors (climate, resources, pollution) |

A common tool to remember the **Macro Environment** is **PESTEL** (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Ecological, Legal).
