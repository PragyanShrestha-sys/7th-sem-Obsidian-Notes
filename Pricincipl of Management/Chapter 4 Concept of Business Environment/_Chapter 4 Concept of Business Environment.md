## [[Business Environment]]

 **Concept of Business Environment:** The dynamic, complex set of internal and external forces (economic, social, political, technological, legal, and competitive) that surround a business, influence its decisions, and require managers to continuously adapt and respond to ensure organizational success.

---
## [[Types of Environment (Internal and External)]]

There are two types of environment:

- **Internal environment:** Factors within the firm's control (e.g., company culture, employees). Example: A manager can change staff training.
- **External environment:** Factors outside the firm's control (e.g., competitors, economic conditions). Example: A manager cannot stop a recession but can adjust pricing.
---
## [[Social Responsibility of Business - Concept and Approaches]]

**Social Responsibility of Business** (also called Corporate Social Responsibility or CSR) refers to the **obligation of a business to protect and serve the interests of society**, not just its own shareholders or owners.

Approaches 

```
Low Responsibility ←---------------------------------------→ High Responsibility

Obstructionist  ---  Defensive  ---  Accommodative  ---  Proactive
(Social Obstruction)  (S.Obligation)    (S.Response)      (S.Contribution)
```

## [[Business Ethics]]

Business ethics refers to the moral principles, values, and standards that guide decision-making and behavior in business. It helps distinguish right from wrong, fair from unfair, and honest from dishonest actions — often going beyond what is legally required.
