## SHORT Q

![[Pasted image 20260620100800.png]]
Here is a concise summary of the 5 data mining primitives:

1. **Task-Relevant Data** – *What data to use.* Specifies the specific dataset, attributes, and filtering conditions (e.g., "US customers in 2025").

2. **Kind of Knowledge** – *What to find.* Defines the mining task, such as association, classification, clustering, or outlier detection.

3. **Background Knowledge** – *Context to guide mining.* Usually in the form of concept hierarchies (e.g., *city → state → country*) to allow different levels of detail.

4. **Interestingness Measures** – *What makes a pattern worthwhile.* Criteria like support, confidence, simplicity, and novelty to filter out trivial results.

5. **Presentation/Visualization** – *How to show results.* Output formats like rules, tables, decision trees, charts, or graphs.

---

**In short:** These primitives act like a "mining query" — they tell the system *where* to look, *what* to look for, *how* to interpret it, *what* to keep, and *how* to show it. Together, they form the basis for a Data Mining Query Language (DMQL).

[[Multidimensional Data Model]]

We need multidimensional data models because **traditional 2D databases (spreadsheets/relational tables) aren't built for the way humans analyze business data.**

**1. Human Intuition**
We naturally think in dimensions: *Product, Time, Region, Customer.* A multidimensional model (like a cube) mirrors this mental map, making analysis intuitive rather than forcing us to decipher complex table joins.

**2. Speed (Pre-Aggregation)**
It calculates totals, averages, and rankings *in advance*. When you ask "sales by region," it doesn't scan millions of rows—it instantly pulls the pre-calculated answer.

**3. "Slicing and Dicing"**
It allows instant, flexible navigation. You can quickly "drill down" (Year → Quarter → Month), "roll up" (City → Country), or "slice" (filter by a specific Product) without writing new code.\

**In one sentence:** We use it to turn massive, chaotic raw data into a structured, high-speed, and easily navigable format that business users can actually understand and query instantly.
