Here are the **things text mining is used on** – short points:

---

## Document & Text Sources

- **Emails** – spam detection, priority sorting
- **Social media posts** – Twitter, Facebook, Reddit, TikTok captions
- **Customer reviews** – Amazon, Yelp, Google Maps reviews
- **News articles** – topic categorization, event tracking
- **Research papers** – literature review, citation analysis
- **Medical records** – doctor's notes, diagnoses, prescriptions
- **Legal documents** – court rulings, contracts, case files
- **Books and e-books** – plagiarism detection, genre classification
- **Chat logs** – customer support conversations, chatbot training
- **Surveys and feedback forms** – open-ended responses
- **Web pages** – search engine indexing, content recommendation
- **Internal company documents** – reports, memos, wikis
- **Job descriptions and resumes** – candidate matching
- **Call center transcripts** – agent performance, common complaints
- **Code comments and documentation** – developer insights
- **Press releases** – corporate sentiment, announcement tracking
- **Patent filings** – prior art search, innovation trends
- **Product descriptions** – e-commerce categorization

---

## Quick Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **User-generated content** | Tweets, reviews, comments, forum posts |
| **Business documents** | Emails, reports, contracts, support tickets |
| **Scientific/medical** | Research papers, clinical notes, lab reports |
| **Legal/government** | Court rulings, laws, public records |
| **News/media** | Articles, blogs, press releases, transcripts |
| **Web content** | Web pages, Wikipedia, online forums |

---

**One-line summary**: *Anywhere there is written human language, text mining can be applied.*
