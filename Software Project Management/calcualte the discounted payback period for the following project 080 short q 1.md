
### Given:
- Discount rate = **10%** per year  
- Initial investment = **Rs. 200,000** (Year 0)

---

### Step 1: Calculate Discounted Cash Flows

Formula:  
**Discounted Cash Flow = Cash Flow / (1 + r)ⁿ**

| Year | Cash Flow | Discount Factor (10%) | Discounted Cash Flow | Cumulative Discounted Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|------------------------|----------------------|--------------------------------|
| 0    | -200,000  | 1.0000                 | **-200,000**         | -200,000                       |
| 1    | 100,000   | 0.9091                 | **90,909**           | -109,091                       |
| 2    | 50,000    | 0.8264                 | **41,322**           | -67,769                        |
| 3    | 50,000    | 0.7513                 | **37,566**           | -30,203                        |

| 5    | 50,000    | 0.6209                 | **31,046**           | +69,144                        |

---

### Step 2: Find the Discounted Payback Period

The initial investment is recovered **during Year 4**.

- Amount needed at start of Year 4 = **Rs. 30,203** (cumulative negative balance)
- Cash flow received in Year 4 = **Rs. 68,301**

Fraction of Year 4 needed:
```
= 30,203 / 68,301
= 0.442 years (≈ 5.3 months)
```

---

### Step 3: Final Answer

**Discounted Payback Period = 3 + 0.442 = 3.44 years**

---

It takes about **3 years and 5 months** to recover the initial investment in today's money (at a 10% discount rate).
