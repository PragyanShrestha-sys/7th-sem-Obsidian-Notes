## The Five Maturity Levels (Technical Explanation)

|Level|Name|Technical Definition|Key Process Areas|
|---|---|---|---|
|**1**|**Initial**|The organization recognizes projects and runs them differently from ongoing business. Processes are **ad hoc**, sometimes even chaotic. Success depends on individual heroics, not repeatable processes.|No formal process areas.|
|**2**|**Repeatable**|Projects are each run in accordance with their **own defined, minimum standards**. Basic project management processes are established to track cost, schedule, and functionality.|Requirements management, project planning, project tracking & oversight, subcontract management, quality assurance, configuration management|
|**3**|**Defined**|The organization has **centrally controlled standards** that are adapted to the needs of individual projects. Standard software processes are documented and integrated across the organization.|Organization process focus, organization process definition, training program, integrated software management, software product engineering, intergroup coordination, peer reviews|
|**4**|**Managed**|The organization routinely obtains **measures of projects' performance**. Detailed metrics are collected and analyzed to achieve quantitative control over software products and processes.|Quantitative process management, software quality management|
|**5**|**Optimized**|The organization runs **continuous improvement** of project management processes with **proactive problem solving**. Defect prevention, technology change management, and process improvement are institutionalized.|Defect prevention, technology change management, process change management|

---

## Summary of the Five Levels

```
Level 1: Initial      → Chaotic, reactive, individual heroics
        ↓
Level 2: Repeatable   → Basic project management, processes repeatable
        ↓
Level 3: Defined      → Standard processes across organization
        ↓
Level 4: Managed      → Measured, predictable, quantitative control
        ↓
Level 5: Optimized    → Continuous improvement, preventive actions
```

## Key Characteristics of Each Level

| Level          | Key Words              | Process Status              | Predictability         |
| -------------- | ---------------------- | --------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| 1 - Initial    | Chaotic, heroic        | Ad-hoc, undocumented        | Unpredictable          |
| 2 - Repeatable | Basic management       | Documented per project      | Repeatable successes   |
| 3 - Defined    | Standardized           | Organization-wide standards | Consistent             |
| 4 - Managed    | Measured               | Quantitative metrics        | Predictable            |
| 5 - Optimized  | Continuous improvement | Feedback-driven improvement | Continuously improving |

## Why is SEI CMM Important?

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Assessment** | Helps organizations understand their current process maturity |
| **Benchmarking** | Provides a standard to compare against industry best practices |
| **Roadmap** | Gives a clear path from chaotic processes to optimized ones |
| **Risk reduction** | Higher maturity levels lead to more predictable project outcomes |
| **Quality improvement** | Higher levels correlate with better software quality |

## Practical Example

| Organization | Maturity Level | Typical Behavior |
|--------------|---------------|------------------|
| **Startup** | Level 1 (Initial) | "We'll figure it out as we go. Hope the launch works!" |
| **Small company with project tracking** | Level 2 (Repeatable) | "We have a project plan and track requirements." |
| **Large enterprise with standards** | Level 3 (Defined) | "All teams follow the same development process." |
| **Mature organization** | Level 4 (Managed) | "We measure defect density and cycle time." |
| **World-class organization** | Level 5 (Optimized) | "We analyze root causes and prevent defects before they happen." |
