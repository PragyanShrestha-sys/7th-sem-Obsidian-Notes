### Significance of PERT Chart (in short)
- **Manages uncertainty** – Uses 3 time estimates (Optimistic, Most Likely, Pessimistic) instead of 1 fixed number.
- **Finds the Critical Path** – Identifies which tasks will delay the whole project if they run late.
- **Helps plan and control** complex projects with many interdependent tasks.

---

### PERT with respect to Z-Values (in short)
- The **Z-value** tells you the **probability** of finishing the project by a target date.
- It measures **how many standard deviations** your target date is away from your expected completion date.

**Interpretation:**

| Z-value | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Z = 0 | 50% chance of meeting the deadline |
| Z = +1 | ~84% chance (safe) |
| Z = -1 | ~16% chance (risky) |

---

**In one line:** PERT handles uncertain timelines, and Z-values convert that uncertainty into a clear **percentage chance** of finishing on time — helping managers make smarter decisions.
