### 1. Definitions

**Break-Even Point (BEP)**  
In general business terms, the break-even point is the level of sales or revenue at which total costs equal total revenues, resulting in zero profit. In **project/investment analysis**, the term is often used to mean the **payback period** (the time it takes to recover the initial investment). It can also refer to the **accounting break-even** (where net income = 0) or **financial break-even** (where NPV = 0), but given your request, we'll treat it as the payback period.

**Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**  
The IRR is the discount rate that makes the Net Present Value (NPV) of all cash flows from a project equal to zero. It represents the expected annual compounded rate of return that the project will generate. A project is generally accepted if its IRR exceeds the required rate of return (cost of capital).

### 2. Payback Period Calculation

The payback period is the time it takes for the cumulative cash flows to recover the initial investment of $50,000.

| Year | Cash Flow | Cumulative Cash Flow |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| 0    | -50,000   | -50,000              |
| 1    | 20,000    | -30,000              |
| 2    | 5,000     | -25,000              |
| 3    | 10,000    | -15,000              |
| 4    | 25,000    | 10,000               |
| 5    | 30,000    | 40,000               |

- After Year 3, we have recovered $35,000, leaving **$15,000** to recover.
- In Year 4, cash inflow is $25,000.

**Payback Period** = 3 years + ($15,000 / $25,000)  
**Payback Period** = 3 + 0.6 = **3.6 years**

---

### 3. ROI (Return on Investment) Calculation

ROI is typically calculated as total net profit divided by the initial investment, expressed as a percentage.

**Total net profit** = Sum of all positive cash flows - Initial investment  
= (20,000 + 5,000 + 10,000 + 25,000 + 30,000) - 50,000  
= 90,000 - 50,000 = **40,000**

**ROI** = (Net Profit / Initial Investment) × 100  
= (40,000 / 50,000) × 100 = **80%**
