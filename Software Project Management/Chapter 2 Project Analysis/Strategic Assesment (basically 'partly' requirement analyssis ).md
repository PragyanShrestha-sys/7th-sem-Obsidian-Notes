Strategic assessment of project analysis ==ensures projects align with organizational goals, evaluating viability, risks, and strategic fit before commitment==. It acts as a gatekeeping process, analyzing the "as-is" state, business needs, and long-term value, rather than just immediate costs, to prioritize resources effectively. Key components include market research, technical feasibility, risk assessment, and stakeholder analysis.

**Key Aspects of Strategic Assessment**

- **Strategic Alignment:** Ensures the project supports the company's long-term goals and strategic direction.
- **Need Analysis:** Defines the "as-is" state, identifies the problem or opportunity, and justifies the need for change.
- **Portfolio/Program View:** Evaluates how the project fits within the wider context of existing company initiatives.
- **Risk and Benefit Analysis:** Examines potential risks and expected benefits, often including sensitivity analysis.
- **Technical Evaluation:** Evaluates if the required technology exists and fits within company capabilities.

[[Major Components and Considerations for strategic assesment]]

**rategic assessment** bridges high-level business strategy with the tactical execution of a software project. In the context of **software project management**, a strategic assessment ensures that the software you’re planning to build isn’t just technically sound, but also aligned with business goals, market realities, and stakeholder needs.
