[[Introduction to Project Analysis]]
Project analysis is ==the systematic evaluation of a project's feasibility, risks, and progress, conducted throughout its life cycle to ensure it stays on track, within budget, and meets objectives==. It involves assessing costs and benefits to make informed decisions and prevent issues before they occur.

---
[[Strategic Assesment (basically 'partly' requirement analyssis )]]
Strategic assessment of project analysis ==ensures projects align with organizational goals, evaluating viability, risks, and strategic fit before commitment==. It acts as a gatekeeping process, analyzing the "as-is" state, business needs, and long-term value, rather than just immediate costs, to prioritize resources effectively. Key components include market research, technical feasibility, risk assessment, and stakeholder analysis.

---
[[Technical Assessment (Technical Fesability 'partly')]]
Technical assessment of project analysis ==evaluates a project's feasibility, examining technical soundness, resource availability, location, and technology selection to ensure operational success==.

Technical assessment in software project management ==evaluates technical feasibility, architecture, and resource requirements to ensure project viability and success==. It involves analyzing technology choices, identifying technical risks, estimating costs, and planning implementation schedules. Key components include assessing hardware/software availability, system design, and security, ultimately determining if the project is actionable. 


---
[[Economic Assessment]]
Economic assessment in software project management ==evaluates a project’s financial viability, determining if expected benefits outweigh costs through techniques like NPV, ROI, and cost-benefit analysis==. It is a critical component of project appraisal, assessing development costs, operational costs, and tangible/intangible benefits to ensure ROI and project feasibility
