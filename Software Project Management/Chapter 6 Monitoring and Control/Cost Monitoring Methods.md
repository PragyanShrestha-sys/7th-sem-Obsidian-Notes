## Cost Monitoring Methods

Based on your slide, here are the five key methods of cost monitoring:

---

### 1. Financial Reporting

**Definition:** Tracking financial data to determine how much money is being spent and earned.

**Purpose:** Track progress and make changes to policies or procedures as needed.

**Example:** Monthly profit and loss statements showing actual vs. budgeted spending.

---

### 2. Budgeting and Forecasting

**Definition:** Estimating future expenses and revenues, then creating a budget that reflects those estimates.

**Purpose:** Track progress and adjust forecasts as new information becomes available.

**Example:** A project budget of $100,000 is revised to $120,000 after discovering additional technical requirements.

---

### 3. Activity Based Costing (ABC)

**Definition:** Assigning a cost to each unit of activity undertaken by a company.

**Purpose:** Track how much money is spent on specific activities and make changes as needed.

**Example:** Assigning a cost of $50 per developer hour to calculate the total cost of building a specific software module.

---

### 4. Cost Segregation

**Definition:** Dividing costs into different categories based on the type of activity involved.

**Purpose:** Identify where costs are increasing or decreasing and make better resource allocation decisions.

**Example:** Separating costs into categories: labor, software licenses, cloud infrastructure, and overhead.

---

### 5. Process Mapping

**Definition:** Mapping out the steps involved in a particular process and assessing how much time is spent on each step.

**Purpose:** Improve efficiency and reduce waste.

**Example:** Mapping the software deployment process to identify that manual testing takes 4 hours per release, leading to automation implementation.

---

### Summary Table

| Method | Focus | Key Benefit |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Financial Reporting** | Tracking actual spend vs. earned | Visibility into financial status |
| **Budgeting & Forecasting** | Estimating future costs | Proactive planning and adjustment |
| **Activity Based Costing** | Cost per activity unit | Granular cost allocation |
| **Cost Segregation** | Categorizing costs | Better resource decisions |
| **Process Mapping** | Time per process step | Efficiency improvement |
