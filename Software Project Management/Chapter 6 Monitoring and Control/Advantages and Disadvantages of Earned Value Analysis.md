

## Advantages of EVA

- **Project Performance Measurement**: Provides a comprehensive method to measure project performance in terms of cost and schedule.

- **Objective Performance Metrics**: Uses objective numerical metrics, reducing subjective interpretation.

- **Integration of Cost and Schedule**: Combines cost and schedule performance, helping managers understand their relationship.

- **Benchmarking**: Allows comparison of project performance with planned targets or previous projects.

- **Effective Communication**: Provides a standardized way to communicate project status to stakeholders through reports and charts.

---

## Disadvantages of EVA

- **Complexity**: Involves complex calculations and terminology, which may be difficult for smaller teams.

- **Resource-Intensive**: Requires detailed data collection and record keeping, which may require additional resources.

- **Assumption of Linear Progression**: Assumes linear project progress, which may not work well for projects with irregular or non-linear development patterns (e.g., research projects).

- **Time-Consuming**: Calculating and updating EVA metrics regularly can be time-consuming.

- **Focus on Metrics Rather than Problems**: Excessive focus on EVA metrics may cause teams to focus on numbers instead of solving the root causes of project problems.
