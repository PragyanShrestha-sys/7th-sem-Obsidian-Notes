In **Software Project Management (SPM)** , **Monitoring and Control** is the process of tracking the progress of a software project, comparing actual performance against the planned baseline, and taking corrective actions when deviations occur.

It is the "doing" and "checking" phase of the **Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA)** cycle. While **Planning** defines _what_ should happen, Monitoring and Control ensures it _actually_ happens according to the constraints of scope, time, cost, and quality.

## [[Major Activities during Monitoring and Control]]

The major Activitites during monitoring and Control are 

| Activity                     | Core Question It Answers                       |
| :--------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- |
| Monitor and Control Work     | Are we on track overall?                       |
| Integrated Change Control    | How do we handle changes properly?             |
| Performance Control Quality  | Is the software good enough?                   |
| Scope Verification & Control | Are we building the right thing—and only that? |
| Control Risk                 | What could go wrong, and is it happening?      |
| Administer Procurement       | Are vendors delivering as promised?            |
