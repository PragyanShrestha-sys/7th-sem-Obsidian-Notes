## Project Control Processes

---

### 1. Establish Standards and Baselines

**Standards** define expectations for team work and behavior. These can be:
- **Qualitative** – e.g., coding best practices, communication norms
- **Quantitative** – e.g., performance metrics, quality thresholds

Standards are documented through:
- Policies
- Procedures
- Templates

**Baseline** is an approved plan (scope, schedule, cost) set during planning. It serves as a **reference point** to track progress.

---

### 2. Measurement

After standards and baselines are set, the project manager measures actual work against them.

**Key Tools:**
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Checklists | Verify task completion and quality checks |
| Dashboards | Visualize real-time status and KPIs |
| Progress Reports | Document achievements, issues, and next steps |

These tools provide status and progress information.

---

### 3. Analysis

The project manager reviews and interprets collected data, comparing it to baselines to **find discrepancies**.

**Key Questions:**
- Are we on schedule?
- Are we within budget?
- What variances exist?
- What are the root causes?

---

### 4. Take Corrective Measures

When discrepancies are found, corrective actions are taken to bring the project back on track.

**Possible Actions:**
- Adjust the plan (revise baselines via change control)
- Communicate with team members
- Reallocate resources
- Implement risk responses

---

## Summary Table

| Step | Process | Key Output |
|------|---------|------------|
| 1 | Establish Standards and Baselines | Approved baselines, policies, templates |
| 2 | Measurement | Checklists, dashboards, progress reports |
| 3 | Analysis | Variance reports, root cause analysis |
| 4 | Corrective Measures | Change requests, revised plans |

---

## Cycle

```
Establish → Measure → Analyze → Correct
     ↑                           ↓
     └───────────────────────────┘
```
