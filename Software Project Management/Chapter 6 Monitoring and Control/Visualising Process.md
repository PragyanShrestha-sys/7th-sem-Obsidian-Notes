### 1. What is Visualizing Progress?

Visualizing progress is the practice of representing collected project data using **graphical or visual formats** (charts, graphs, dashboards) to communicate:
- **Current status:** Where are we right now?
- **Past performance:** How did we get here?
- **Future projections:** Where are we likely to end up?

### 3. Common Visualization Methods (Expanded)

Your slide lists four commonly used methods. Here is a detailed explanation of each, along with when to use them:

#### A. [[Gantt Chart]]

A **Gantt Chart** is a horizontal bar chart that visually represents a project schedule over time. It is one of the most widely used tools in project management for planning, scheduling, and monitoring progress.

- **Tasks** and their durations
- **Timeline** (start and end dates)
- **Dependencies** between tasks
- **Progress** (actual vs. planned)
- **Milestones** (key events or deliverables)

---
#### B. [[Slip Chart]]

A **Slip Chart** (also known as a **Schedule Slippage Chart**) is a visual project management tool used to track and report schedule delays. In software project management, where requirements often change and estimation is difficult, the Slip Chart serves as an early warning system to detect when a project is falling behind its planned timeline.

### Summary

**Visualizing Progress** is the bridge between raw project data and actionable insight. It transforms complex, scattered information into clear, intuitive graphics that enable teams and stakeholders to:

- Quickly understand current status
- Spot trends, bottlenecks, and delays
- Make data-driven decisions
- Maintain accountability and alignment

Methods like **Gantt Charts**, **Slip Charts**, **Ball Charts**, and **Timeline Charts** each serve specific purposes, from detailed schedule tracking to executive-level reporting. In modern software project management, these are often complemented by Agile-focused visuals like burndown charts and cumulative flow diagrams to provide a comprehensive view of project health.
