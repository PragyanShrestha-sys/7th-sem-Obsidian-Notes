## Elements / Components of Contract Management

The image identifies four core components:

### 1. Contract Creation
**What it is:** The initial development of the contract document before it is signed.  
**Activities:**
- Drafting terms based on negotiations.
- Defining scope, deliverables, timeline, payment, ownership, and acceptance criteria.
- Including legal and technical specifications.  
*Note: This overlaps with contract placement but is listed here as the first component of the management lifecycle.*

### 2. Contract Execution
**What it is:** The formal activation of the contract.  
**Activities:**
- Obtaining signatures from authorized representatives of both parties.
- Issuing a "Notice to Proceed" to the vendor.
- Distributing executed copies to all stakeholders.
- Setting up contract files and tracking systems.

### 3. Contract Administration
**What it is:** The day-to-day oversight of the contract during project execution.  
**Activities:**
- Monitoring ***deliverables and milestones*** against the timetable.
- Processing and tracking invoices and payments.
- Managing change orders (formal amendments to scope, cost, or schedule).
- Documenting communications, ***approvals, and issues.***
- Handling non-performance (e.g., cure notices, penalty application).
- Conducting progress reviews and quality audits.

### 4. Contract Analysis
**What it is:** Evaluating contract performance and outcomes, both during and after the project.  
**Activities:**
- Comparing actual vendor performance against contractual commitments.
- Analyzing cost variances, schedule delays, or quality deviations.
- Assessing risk exposure and compliance gaps.
- Performing lessons learned for future procurement.
- Preparing contract close-out reports.

## Objectives of Contract Management

The image lists four primary objectives:

### 1. Ensure compliance with contract terms
- Verify that both parties follow all clauses (timelines, deliverables, standards, payment schedules).
- Confirm that the vendor uses agreed-upon technologies, security practices, and reporting methods.
- Ensure the client provides timely feedback, access, and resources as committed.

### 2. Achieve project goals efficiently
- Align contract execution with project milestones (e.g., completing sprints, passing acceptance tests).
- Avoid delays caused by administrative bottlenecks or misunderstandings.
- Use the contract as a roadmap, not just a legal document.

### 3. Minimize risks and conflicts
- Identify potential issues early (e.g., missed deadlines, quality shortfalls).
- Manage change requests through formal contract amendments to prevent scope creep.
- Reduce disputes by maintaining clear records of communications, deliverables, and approvals.

### 4. Improve performance and accountability
- Track vendor performance against key indicators (e.g., on-time delivery, defect rates).
- Hold both parties accountable for their responsibilities.
- Use performance data to inform future contract negotiations or vendor selections.
