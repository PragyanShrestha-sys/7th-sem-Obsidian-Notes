
## Leadership Styles

Leadership styles describe the **approach a leader uses to make decisions and manage team members**. These styles are often analyzed based on **two dimensions (axes)**:

| Axis | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Directive** | Leader closely **supervises and controls** implementation. Tells team members exactly what to do and how to do it. |
| **Permissive** | Leader gives **freedom in execution**. Team members decide how to accomplish goals. |
| **Autocratic** | Leader makes **decisions alone** without input from the team. |
| **Democratic** | Decisions are made with **team participation**. Leader seeks input and builds consensus. |

---

## Combining the Axes: Four Leadership Styles

By combining these two dimensions, we get four common leadership styles:

| Style                     | Decision-Making (Autocratic → Democratic) | Execution Control (Directive → Permissive) | Description                                                                                                |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Autocratic-Directive**  | Autocratic (alone)                        | Directive (tight control)                  | Leader decides everything and closely supervises every step. Useful in crises or with inexperienced teams. |
| **Autocratic-Permissive** | Autocratic (alone)                        | Permissive (freedom)                       | Leader makes decisions alone but gives team freedom in how to execute. Rare; can be demotivating.          |
| **Democratic-Directive**  | Democratic (team input)                   | Directive (tight control)                  | Leader involves team in decisions but then closely controls execution. Uncommon balance.                   |
| **Democratic-Permissive** | Democratic (team input)                   | Permissive (freedom)                       | Leader facilitates team decisions and trusts team to execute freely. Common in Agile software teams.       |

> **Note:** The most common and effective style in software projects is often **Democratic-Permissive** (also called "servant leadership" or "facilitative leadership"), especially for experienced, self-organizing Agile teams.

## Practical Advice for Software Project Managers

| Situation | Recommended Style |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Team is new, inexperienced, or facing a crisis | Autocratic-Directive (clear direction, tight control) |
| Team is experienced and self-organizing (e.g., Agile) | Democratic-Permissive (trust and autonomy) |
| Critical technical decision with high risk | Democratic-Directive (get team input, but leader ensures correct execution) |
| Routine, low-risk task | Autocratic-Permissive (leader decides, team executes freely) |

> **Takeaway:** No single style is always best. Effective leaders adapt their style based on the team's maturity, the project's risk, and the organizational culture.
