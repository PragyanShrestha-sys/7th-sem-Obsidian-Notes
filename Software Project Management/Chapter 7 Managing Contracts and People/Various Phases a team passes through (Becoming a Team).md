## Core Idea

Simply throwing people together does not mean they will immediately be able to work together as a team. Teamwork develops gradually over time as members interact, resolve conflicts, and build trust. The following model describes the five stages a group evolves through to become a high-performing team.

---

## The Five Stages of Team Development

| Stage | Description | Key Characteristics |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **1. Forming** | Team members are testing each other and exploring where they fit in the project. | - Members worry about how they fit in with others <br>- Concern about how their capabilities and skills compare <br>- Looking to the **project manager for clarity and direction** |
| **2. Storming** | Struggles for power and to determine how the team will work together. | - Differences of opinion are common <br>- Team tries to determine how to work together effectively <br>- Conflicts arise over roles, processes, and leadership |
| **3. Norming** | Individuals begin to see how they form a team and work together effectively. | - Set rules and group norms to work by <br>- Learned how to resolve differences of opinion <br>- Becoming much more comfortable with each other <br>- Trusting each other to get the job done |
| **4. Performing** | The team is performing consistently at a high level. | - Focused on reaching project goals as a team <br>- **The individual does not exist**; team members are interdependent <br>- Can solve problems on their own <br>- Do not rely on the project manager for direction |
| **5. Adjourning** | The team completes the project and disbands. | - Feeling of loss about having to move on to other projects <br>- Particularly common with very high-performing teams <br>- Members have learned to work effectively and will miss each other |



> **Takeaway:** Rushing or skipping stages (especially Storming) leads to fragile teams. Allow time for each stage, and recognize that high-performing teams often feel a genuine sense of loss when disbanding.
