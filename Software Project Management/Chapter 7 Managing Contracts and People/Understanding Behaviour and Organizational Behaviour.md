## Understanding Behaviour

### Core Idea
Effective and sensitive management of staff comes only from **experience**. Identifying and handling people and their behavior is a very important aspect of project management.

### Two Approaches to Understanding Behavior

| Approach                    | Description                                                                                                                                                          | Example                                                                                                                                                              |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Positivist Approach**     | Based on development of systems and discipline of organizational theory. Follows a cause-and-effect model: **"If A situation happens, then B is likely to result."** | "If a developer misses two deadlines in a row (A), then they need additional training or mentoring (B)."                                                             |
| **Interpretivist Approach** | A more qualitative approach that views **each situation as unique and non-predictive**. Focuses on context and human subjectivity rather than fixed rules.           | Instead of applying a standard policy, the manager talks to the developer to understand personal issues causing the delay, recognizing that every case is different. |

> **Takeaway:** No single formula works for all people or all situations. Good managers blend both approaches—using predictable systems where possible but adapting to unique circumstances when needed.

## Organizational Behaviour

### Historical Background
In the late 19th and early 20th century, **Frederick Taylor** attempted to analyze the most productive way of doing manual tasks. His work laid the foundation for organizational behavior studies.

### [[Taylor's Basic Objectives]]
1. **Select the best man for the job** – Match skills to tasks.
2. **Instruct them in the best method** – Provide clear, efficient procedures.
3. **Give incentives in the form of higher wages to the best workers** – Reward productivity to motivate performance.

### Key Finding
Later organizational behaviour research found that the **state of minds of people** (their attitudes, emotions, and perceptions) helps in better productivity—not just physical efficiency or financial incentives.

> **Takeaway:** Productivity in software projects depends as much on **how people feel** (motivated, valued, understood) as on their technical skills or following processes.

## Summary Table

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| Positivist Approach | Predictable cause-effect rules for behavior |
| Interpretivist Approach | Every situation is unique; adapt accordingly |
| Taylor's Focus | Select best person, teach best method, reward with wages |
| Modern Finding | State of mind (emotions, attitudes) drives productivity |

In software project management, understanding both behavioral approaches helps you manage developers, testers, and stakeholders more effectively—blending structure with empathy.
