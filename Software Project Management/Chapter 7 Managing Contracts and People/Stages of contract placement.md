Based on the image you provided, here is an explanation of the **six stages of contract placement** in software project management, as listed in your document.

These stages describe the sequential process from receiving a vendor's initial offer to formally closing the contract after work is complete.

---

### 1. Proposal Stage

**What happens:** The software vendor responds to the client's Request for Proposal (RFP) or Request for Quote (RFQ) by submitting a formal proposal.

**Key activities:**
- Vendor analyzes the client's Statement of Work (SOW).
- Vendor proposes technical solution, methodology (e.g., Agile, Waterfall), team composition, timeline, and pricing.
- Client evaluates proposals against predefined criteria (cost, technical fit, experience).

**Output:** A shortlist of qualified vendors or a single preferred proposal.

---

### 2. Negotiation Stage

**What happens:** The client and selected vendor discuss and align on terms that are not fixed in the proposal.

**Key activities:**
- Negotiate price, payment schedule, and delivery milestones.
- Clarify scope boundaries (what is included vs. what is a change request).
- Discuss intellectual property ownership (e.g., who owns source code).
- Agree on warranties, liability limits, penalties for delay, and dispute resolution.

**Output:** A mutually agreed set of commercial and legal terms.

---

### 3. Contract Drafting Stage

**What happens:** The agreed terms are converted into a formal, legally binding written document.

**Key activities:**
- Draft the contract including: SOW, terms and conditions, payment schedule, acceptance criteria, confidentiality clauses, and termination provisions.
- Incorporate all negotiation outcomes.
- Use templates (e.g., Master Service Agreement) or create a bespoke contract.

**Output:** A complete draft contract ready for review.

---

### 4. Review and Approval Stage

**What happens:** Both parties' legal, technical, and financial teams review the draft before final commitment.

**Key activities:**
- Legal review for compliance, risk exposure, and enforceability.
- Technical review to ensure SOW matches actual project needs.
- Financial review of payment terms and budget alignment.
- Internal approvals (e.g., project sponsor, procurement department, CTO).

**Output:** A final, approved contract with all revisions incorporated.

---

### 5. Execution Stage

**What happens:** The contract becomes legally binding through formal signing.

**Key activities:**
- Both parties sign the contract (physically or via e-signature).
- Exchange executed copies.
- Issue a Notice to Proceed (NTP) authorizing the vendor to start software development.

**Output:** A fully executed, enforceable contract.

---

### 6. Closure Stage

**What happens:** The contract is formally closed after all deliverables are accepted and obligations met.

**Key activities:**
- Final acceptance of software product (sign-off on user acceptance testing).
- Resolve any outstanding payments or claims.
- Transfer documentation (source code, passwords, architecture diagrams).
- Conduct lessons learned or procurement audit.
- Release any performance bonds or retainage.

**Output:** Contract closure notice; vendor relationship may continue under a new contract or maintenance agreement.

---

These six stages ensure that software contracts are placed systematically, reducing legal risk and misalignment before development begins.
