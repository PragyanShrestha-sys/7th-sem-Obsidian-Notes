[[Chapter 3 Acitivity Planning and Scheduling/Introduction]]
Activity planning is the process of defining all specific tasks required to complete a project, typically starting with a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and refining details as more information becomes available. Scheduling builds on this by sequencing the identified activities in a logical order—using relationships like finish-to-start—and estimating resources and durations to create a timeline. Together, they form the foundation of a project schedule, ensuring tasks are clearly identified, logically organized, and efficiently executed within project constraints.

---
[[Work Breakdown Structure ]]
A **Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)** is a hierarchical decomposition of a project into smaller, more manageable components. It breaks the overall project work into phases, deliverables, and individual activities or tasks, making it easier to plan, execute, and track progress.

the WBS serves as the foundation for activity planning by ensuring that no critical work is overlooked. Each level provides increasing detail, allowing for clearer assignment of responsibilities, more accurate time and cost estimates, and better control over the project scope.

---
[[Bar Chart or Gantt Chart]]

A **Gantt chart** (also called a bar chart in this context) is a visual project management tool that displays activities or tasks against time. It shows **what needs to be done**, **who should do it**, **when it should start and finish**, and **how tasks relate to one another**.

---
[[Network Planning Model (PERT and CPM)]]

A **network planning model** is a graphical technique used to represent, analyze, and manage the sequence of activities in a project. It shows **logical dependencies** between tasks and helps determine the **project duration**, **critical path**, and **schedule flexibility**.

---
[[Precedence Diagramming Method]]
The **Precedence Diagramming Method (PDM)** is a visual project management technique used to construct network diagrams where **activities are represented as nodes (boxes)** and **dependencies are shown as arrows** connecting them. It is the most commonly used method for creating project schedules.
