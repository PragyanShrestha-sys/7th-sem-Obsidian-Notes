## Part 2: Steps in Risk Classification and Analysis (yo Basically table banako ho for 'final result (probability * impact)' nikalna  )

The image outlines **6 clear steps**:

### Step 1: Identify Risk Problems
Determine the problems that may cause risks in the project.

**Example:** *"Changes to requirements specification during coding"*

---

### Step 2: Identify Probability
Estimate the likelihood of each risk occurring.

**Scale options:**
- Qualitative: Low / Moderate / Significant / High
- Quantitative: 1–10 or 0–100%

---

### Step 3: Identify Impact
Assess the severity or damage if the risk occurs.

**Scale options:**
- Qualitative: Low / Moderate / Significant / High
- Quantitative: 1–10 or monetary value ($)

---

### Step 4: Assign Values
Rate probability and impact on a scale (e.g., 1–10 or Low/Medium/High).

---

### Step 5: Calculate Risk Exposure (RE)

**Formula:**
```
Risk Exposure (RE) = Probability × Impact
```

- RE may be calculated in **monetary** or **numerical** terms.
- Higher RE means higher priority.

---

### Step 6: Prioritize Risks
Prepare a table of risks and rank them based on Risk Exposure.

**Higher Risk Exposure → Higher Priority**

## Bottom Line

**Risk Classification and Analysis** turns a raw list of risks into a **prioritized ranking** using:
- **Probability levels** (High/Significant/Moderate/Low)
- **Impact levels** (High/Significant/Moderate/Low)
- **Risk Exposure formula** (Probability × Impact)

> **Key takeaway:** Higher Risk Exposure = Higher Priority. Focus your response planning on risks with the highest RE scores.
