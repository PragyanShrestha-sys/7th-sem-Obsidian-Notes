Software engineering problems in project management are ==core challenges arising from the technical complexity, uncertainty, and constant change inherent in developing software, often causing projects to exceed budgets, miss deadlines, and produce low-quality products==. Key issues include poor communication, scope creep, unrealistic stakeholder expectations, and difficulty managing technical debt
**Key Software Engineering Problems in Project Management**

- **Requirements Volatility & Ambiguity:** Constantly changing or poorly defined requirements are a major issue, causing scope creep and misalignment between developer output and stakeholder expectations.

- **Managing High Complexity:** As systems grow, managing the intricate details of software architecture becomes difficult, often leading to unforeseen bugs, technical debt, and integration issues.

- **Time and Resource Constraints:** Tight schedules, often aggravated by inaccurate estimation, lead to poor quality, burnout, and skipped testing phases.

- **Communication & Collaboration Issues:** Distributed teams or poor coordination between developers, stakeholders, and users lead to misunderstandings, integration failures, and project delays.

- **Technology Uncertainty:** Using new or unfamiliar technologies can lead to performance issues, security risks, and unexpected technical obstacles.

- **Software Deterioration & Technical Debt:** Failure to maintain code quality leads to systems that are difficult to update, enhance, or repair over time.
