**1. Information Systems vs. Embedded Systems**

Think of this as the difference between software that *helps people work* and software that *runs a machine*.

- **Information systems** are like digital office assistants. They help people do tasks like writing reports, managing schedules, or handling customer data. If the software stops, office work becomes slower or impossible. Examples: payroll software, hospital patient trackers, Microsoft Excel.

- **Embedded systems** are like the hidden brain inside a device. You don't "use" them like an app — they just run automatically to control hardware. If the software fails, the machine stops working. Examples: the software in your car's braking system, a microwave's control panel, or a drone's flight controller.

---

**2. Outsourced Projects**

Imagine you're building a huge house. Instead of doing everything yourself, you hire an electrician for the wiring and a plumber for the pipes. That's exactly what outsourcing is in software.

When a project is too big, too complex, or needs specialized skills, a company will pay another company to build certain pieces. The main company still manages the overall project, but the external team delivers specific parts.

Example: A bank builds its own core system but hires an outside firm to design the mobile banking app.

---

**3. Object-driven Software Projects**

This one is simple: the software is built for **one specific goal** and nothing else.

It's like buying a toaster — it only toasts bread. You can't use it to boil water or play music. Similarly, object-driven software solves a very narrow problem and doesn't try to be flexible or general-purpose.

Example: A program that only converts video files from MP4 to AVI. It does one job, and it does it well.

---

**4. Compulsory vs. Voluntary Software**

This is about **choice** — whether someone is forced to use the software or decides to use it for fun or convenience.

- **Compulsory software** = no real choice. You have to use it to get your job done. If you refuse, you can't complete your work.  
  Example: A cashier must use the store's point-of-sale system. A student must use the university's exam portal.

- **Voluntary software** = pure choice. You download or use it because you want to, not because someone is forcing you. Stop using it, and nothing bad happens.  
  Example: Spotify for music, Instagram for social media, or a chess game on your phone.
