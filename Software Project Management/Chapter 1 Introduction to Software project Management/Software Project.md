**Key Characteristics of a Software Project:**

- **Unique Goal:** Aims to create a specific software application, update, or solution.
- **Temporary Nature:** Has a defined start and end date.
- **Constraint-Driven:** Subject to budget, time, and quality limitations.
- **Non-Physical Asset:** Focuses on developing logical instructions (software) rather than physical hardware, which often makes progress less tangible.
- **High Risk/Change:** Due to evolving technology and specific user requirements, these projects often face technical challenges and changing requirements, requiring constant, iterative management.

**Key Phases (Software Project Management):**

1. **Initiation:** Defining the project scope and feasibility.
2. **Planning:** Estimating resources, costs, and scheduling.
3. **Execution:** Developing and implementing the software.
4. **Monitoring & Control:** Tracking progress and managing risks.
5. **Closure:** Delivering the final product
