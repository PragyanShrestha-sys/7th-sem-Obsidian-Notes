Software project management covers the entire lifecycle to ensure successful delivery within constraints, focusing on three key stages: ==feasibility studies (assessing viability), planning (defining scope, budget, and schedule), and execution (implementation and monitoring)==. These activities ensure cost-benefit analysis, risk management, and quality control, using tools like Work Breakdown Structures (WBS) to manage resources effectively.

**Key Activities Covered:**

- Fesability Study
    - **Technical Feasibility:** Assessing if the technology exists or can be developed to meet requirements.
    - **Economic/Financial Feasibility:** Conducting cost-benefit analysis to determine if the project is financially viable.
    - **Operational Feasibility:** Evaluating if the proposed system will solve the business problem and fit within the current organization.
    - **Project Selection:** Reviewing options to maximize project portfolio value.
- Project Planning 
    - **Scope Definition:** Defining the project boundaries and Creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).
    - **Scheduling:** Identifying tasks, dependencies, and developing a timeline (e.g., Gantt charts).
    - **Resource Planning:** Allocating team members, tools, and budget.
    - **Risk Management: Identifying potential risks and planning mitigation strategies.**
- Project Execution
    - **Development & Implementation:** Actual creation of the software product through design and coding.
    - **Team Coordination & Management:** Assigning tasks and ensuring effective communication.
    - **Monitoring & Control:** Tracking progress against the plan, controlling budget expenditure, and managing risks.
    - **Quality Management:** Overseeing testing, code reviews, and quality assurance to meet project standards.
